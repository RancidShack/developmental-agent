# v1.0 Pre-Registration: Integration Audit of the Cumulative Inheritance Chain via Heavy Instrumentation at Cross-Layer Coupling Moments

**Author:** Nicholas P M Baker, Synapstak Ltd
**Date:** 27 April 2026
**Status:** Pre-registration document, committed before any v1.0 instrumentation code is written

---

## 1. Scope and inheritance

This document pre-registers v1.0, the integration paper that audits the cumulative inheritance chain across v0.8 through v0.14. v1.0 is not a new architectural iteration. It introduces no new architectural mechanism, no new cell type, no new drive, no new action-selection rule. The architecture v1.0 audits is the v0.14 architecture as specified in Baker, 2026m and amended at v0.14.1 (Baker, 2026n) — the same architecture, run under the same experimental matrix, with seeds matched to the existing inheritance chain.

What v1.0 contributes is an audit that the existing iteration papers' per-run CSVs cannot perform on their own. The contribution has two components. The first is a direct full-architecture-versus-baseline characterisation across the cumulative inheritance chain — running v0.14 under matched seeds against every preceding iteration with a published baseline (v0.8, v0.10, v0.11.2, v0.12, v0.13), extending the pairwise matched-seed comparisons the iteration papers have produced into a full-arc audit. The second is heavy instrumentation that captures per-state snapshots at coupling moments, producing data the existing per-run CSVs do not carry: the drive-weighted scores, Q-values, primitive bias, preferences, gate state, flag state, and cell-type state at the precise moments when cross-layer coupling fires. The instrumented data lets v1.0 characterise the bidirectional coupling identified in v0.14 mechanistically rather than correlationally.

The architectural arc the integration paper documents has two parts that v1.0 holds together. The first arc, v0.1 through v0.8, established the five behavioural signatures associated with developmental learning and produced the foundational individuation finding at v0.8. That arc is documented in the v0.8 essay (Baker, 2026b) and the v0.8 formal paper (Baker, 2026a). The second arc, v0.9 through v0.14, addressed the architectural fragility v0.9 surfaced and built persistent categorical knowledge (v0.10 threat-side, v0.11.2 mastery-side), category-level generalisation (v0.12), late-target activation (v0.13), and competency-gated content transformation (v0.14). v1.0's audit covers both arcs: the v0.8 individuation finding preserved through to v0.14 inheritance, and the v0.9 → v0.14 architectural extensions composed into a coherent whole.

## 2. Architectural state at v1.0

The architecture audited in v1.0 is the v0.14 architecture. No code changes are made beyond the addition of instrumentation. Specifically:

- The five cell types from v0.14 are preserved: FRAME, NEUTRAL, HAZARD, ATTRACTOR, END_STATE, KNOWLEDGE.
- The v0.14 cell-type transition rule is preserved: hazard cells transition to knowledge cells when the agent's competency reaches each cell's assigned threshold.
- The v0.14 action-selection hard-gate exception is preserved: the gate excludes flagged hazard cells and admits flagged knowledge cells.
- The v0.14 banking rules are preserved for both attractor cells (three-entry banking, four mastery interventions) and knowledge cells (three-entry banking, four mastery interventions plus threat-flag clearing).
- The v0.14 amended end-state activation trigger is preserved: `all_attractors_mastered AND all_hazards_banked_as_knowledge`.
- All inherited mechanisms from v0.10 (persistent threat representation, three-entry conversion), v0.11.2 (four mastery interventions), v0.12 (signature-matching first-entry conversion), and v0.13 (random-location end-state cell, one-entry banking) are preserved.
- The three-phase developmental schedule, drive composition at each phase, learning rate 0.1, discount factor 0.9, and epsilon 0.1 are inherited unchanged.
- The pre-wired aversion bias of −5.0 for frame-adjacent actions is retained.

The single-variable-change discipline at the architectural level is satisfied trivially: v1.0 introduces no architectural change. The single-variable-change at the methodological level introduces one thing: heavy instrumentation at coupling moments. The instrumentation does not modify the agent's behaviour. It records state at specified moments without altering the action-selection pipeline, the update rules, or any other architectural element.

## 3. Heavy instrumentation specification

### 3.1 Coupling moments

The instrumentation captures state at six classes of coupling moment, each defined unambiguously in terms of architectural events:

- **Attractor banking:** the step at which an attractor cell's mastery flag is first set (third entry to the cell). Five such moments per run if all attractors are banked.
- **Cell-type transition:** the step at which a hazard cell transitions to a knowledge cell (competency reaches the cell's assigned threshold). Up to five such moments per run.
- **Knowledge banking:** the step at which a knowledge cell is banked (third entry post-transition). Up to five such moments per run.
- **Threat-flag clearing at knowledge banking:** the step at which a persistent threat flag is cleared at the moment of knowledge banking. Co-occurs with knowledge banking but recorded separately for analysis.
- **End-state activation:** the step at which the v0.14 amended end-state activation trigger first fires. At most one such moment per run.
- **End-state banking:** the step at which the end-state cell is banked (first entry post-activation). At most one such moment per run.

The maximum number of coupling moments per run is bounded by 5 + 5 + 5 + 5 + 1 + 1 = 22. Most runs will produce fewer. At each coupling moment, the instrumentation captures state at the moment itself, the step before, and the step after. The total per-run snapshot count is bounded by 66.

### 3.2 Snapshot content

At each snapshot, the instrumentation records:

- **Step number.**
- **Coupling moment class** (one of the six classes above; "step before" and "step after" snapshots inherit the class of the central event).
- **Agent's current cell coordinate.**
- **Drive-weighted scores per candidate action.** Four values: the drive-weighted score for each of the four cardinal actions at the action-selection step. The drive-weighted score is the sum of (drive weight) × (drive value) across all active drives for the resulting cell of each candidate action.
- **Q-values per candidate action.** Four values: the Q(s, a) values for each cardinal action.
- **Primitive bias per candidate action.** Four values: the primitive bias contribution to each cardinal action's selection. Includes the −5.0 frame-adjacent aversion, the +0.3 ATTRACTION_BONUS for adjacent unbanked attractor / end-state / knowledge cells, and any other primitive bias terms.
- **Preferences over the relevant cell set.** The preference value for the agent's current cell, all four adjacent cells, all six attractor cells, all five hazard / knowledge cells (whichever type each is at the snapshot moment), and the end-state cell if sampled. Approximately 16–17 values per snapshot.
- **Gate exclusion set.** The set of cells currently excluded by the action-selection hard gate. Bounded by the number of hazard cells (five), since only flagged-hazard cells are excluded.
- **Threat-flag state per cell.** The threat flag (0 or 1) for each of the five hazard / knowledge cells. Five values.
- **Cell-type state per cell.** The current cell type for each of the five hazard / knowledge cells (HAZARD or KNOWLEDGE), the six attractor cells (ATTRACTOR or banked-ATTRACTOR), and the end-state cell (NEUTRAL or END_STATE). Twelve values.

Per-snapshot data volume is approximately 50 floats plus 22 categorical values, totalling roughly 0.5 KB. Per-run data volume is bounded at 66 × 0.5 KB ≈ 33 KB. Total batch data volume across 180 runs is bounded at approximately 6 MB. The data volume is tractable; the analysis surface is bounded.

### 3.3 Implementation discipline

The instrumentation is implemented as a separate recording layer on top of the v0.14 codebase. The agent's action-selection pipeline is not modified; the recording layer is called after each action is selected and before each step is executed, with read-only access to the agent's state. The recording layer is enabled by a configuration flag; with the flag off, the v0.14 batch produces bit-for-bit identical output to the existing v0.14 batches. The flag must be on for the v1.0 batch.

The implementation surface is contained: a recording-layer module, integration with the agent's main loop at six event-detection points, and an output writer that serialises snapshot data to a separate CSV. No modification to `curiosity_agent_v0_14.py` beyond the integration calls. The single-pass-without-architectural-amendment expectation that has held across the chain applies.

## 4. Experimental matrix and matched-seed comparison

### 4.1 Matrix

The experimental matrix is one architecture (v0.14 with v1.0 instrumentation, permutation_offset = 0) crossed with six cost levels (0.1, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 5.0, 10.0) crossed with three run lengths (20,000, 80,000, 160,000 steps) crossed with ten runs per cell, totalling 180 runs.

### 4.2 Seed chain

For each (cost, run length, run index) triple, the random seed is matched to the existing inheritance chain at the same condition. Seeds are loaded from `run_data_v0_14.csv` (Baker, 2026m batch), which inherits from v0.13, which inherits from v0.12, v0.11.2, and v0.10 in turn. The seed chain links v0.8, v0.10, v0.11.2, v0.12, v0.13, v0.14, and v1.0 at every (cost, run length, run index) cell.

### 4.3 Comparison apparatus

v1.0 produces matched-seed comparisons against six baselines:

- **v0.8** for individuation preservation (Category γ).
- **v0.10** for threat-layer preservation (Category α component).
- **v0.11.2** for mastery preservation and the original mastery-affects-threat-side cross-layer effect (Category α and β components).
- **v0.12** for category-level generalisation preservation (Category α component).
- **v0.13** for late-target activation preservation and the end-state-affects-threat-side cross-layer effect (Category α and β components).
- **v0.14** for full-architecture preservation against the immediately preceding iteration (Category α component).

The comparisons use the recorded per-run CSVs from each baseline, plus the v0.8 batch's published metrics. No re-run of any prior iteration is performed.

## 5. Metrics

### 5.1 Inherited metrics

All v0.10, v0.11.2, v0.12, v0.13, and v0.14 metrics are retained: phase boundaries, frame attempts by phase, hazard entries by phase, threat-layer timing metrics, first-entry conversions, attractors mastered, mastery timing, mastery sequence, end-state cell location, end-state activation step, end-state found step, end-state visits, end-state banked, and the v0.14 metrics for transition steps, knowledge entries, knowledge banking steps, knowledge banking flags, and hazards banked as knowledge.

### 5.2 Snapshot metrics

The instrumentation produces per-snapshot records as specified in Section 3.2. The per-snapshot CSV is the primary output of the v1.0 instrumentation. Aggregate analyses are produced from this CSV during the analysis stage, including the derived metrics in Section 5.3.

### 5.3 Derived metrics for Category β

The mechanistic characterisation of cross-layer coupling rests on three derived metric families.

**Coupling magnitude.** For each coupling moment, the change in drive-weighted score across the snapshot triple (before, at-moment, after) for the action that targets the cell at the heart of the coupling event. A transition moment, for example, produces a drive-weighted-score trajectory for the action targeting the transitioned cell across the three snapshots; the magnitude of the score change is the local-coupling-strength estimate at that moment.

**Coupling direction.** Per coupling moment, whether the agent's selected action immediately following the moment targets the cell at the heart of the event (positive direction), targets a cell elsewhere (negative direction), or follows an unrelated trajectory (neutral direction). Across the population of coupling moments, the directional distribution characterises whether coupling reliably draws the agent toward the coupling event.

**Cross-layer state correlation.** Per coupling moment, whether state changes at the moment correlate across layers as the bidirectional-coupling reading predicts. Specifically: at attractor banking moments, whether the gate exclusion set, threat-flag state, and cell-type state for hazard cells are unchanged (mastery-side independence) or whether they show systematic change (cross-layer effect at attractor banking). At cell-type transition moments, whether the gate exclusion set updates atomically with the cell-type change. At knowledge banking moments, whether threat-flag clearing co-occurs precisely with banking.

These derived metrics constitute the empirical content of Category β.

### 5.4 Derived metrics for Category γ

Individuation preservation rests on three diversity measures applied to the v1.0 batch and compared against v0.8's published distributions:

- **Top-attractor diversity.** The number of distinct attractor cells appearing as primary focus across the ten runs per condition, summed across conditions. v0.8 reported six of six attractors appearing across ten runs at the relevant condition; the v1.0 measure is the count of distinct top attractors per condition under matched seeds.
- **Mastery-sequence diversity.** The number of distinct mastery sequences (ordered tuples of attractor banking events) across the ten runs per condition. v0.14 reported substantial mastery-sequence diversity at long run lengths; v1.0 reproduces the measure under instrumentation.
- **Knowledge-banking-order diversity.** The number of distinct knowledge-banking orders across the ten runs per condition for runs in which all five hazards are banked as knowledge. New to v1.0; not previously measured at the per-condition level.

The Category γ verdict rests on whether the diversity measures v1.0 produces are consistent with v0.8's foundational individuation finding extended across the inheritance chain.

## 6. Pre-registered interpretation categories

The category structure for v1.0 differs from the iteration papers' Category A–F structure. v1.0 is an integration audit, not a new mechanism's operational test. The structure separates empirical claims (Categories α through δ) from interpretive claims (Category Ω) explicitly.

### 6.1 Category α (preservation)

Category α tests whether the v1.0 batch's full-architecture run preserves the headline findings of each preceding iteration at matched seeds. Five sub-checks:

**α-1 (v0.8 baseline):** Five v0.8 signatures preserved. Specifically: rule adherence (zero hazard entries during Phase 3 in runs that have flagged the relevant hazards) holds at the v0.10-or-better rate; concentration in Phase 3 (top-attractor concentration index) holds at the v0.8 rate or better; feature-aligned preference formation (feature cells appearing in the agent's top preferences) holds at the v0.7.1-or-better rate; specialist-generalist trade-off visible in mastery-sequence diversity; biographical individuation visible in top-attractor diversity per condition. The first four are quantitative; individuation is the qualitative-into-quantitative measure Section 5.4 specifies.

**α-2 (v0.10 baseline):** Persistent threat representation operates as v0.10 specifies. Specifically: the three-entry conversion rule fires for the first hazard cell encountered; the persistent flag holds across Phase 3; the cost-economics finding from v0.10 (50–75% reduction in mean total cost relative to v0.9) holds in v1.0.

**α-3 (v0.11.2 baseline):** Bounded mastery operates as v0.11.2 specifies. Specifically: the four mastery interventions hold for attractor cells; MASTERY_THRESHOLD = 3 fires correctly; mastery flags persist; the v0.11.2 individuation findings (mastery-sequence diversity at long run lengths) hold in v1.0.

**α-4 (v0.12 baseline):** Category-level generalisation operates as v0.12 specifies. Specifically: signature-matching converts hazard cells on first entry after the first cell's three-entry conversion; the v0.10 boundary condition (5/10 Phase 3 cleanness at cost 5.0, 20,000 steps) is eliminated as v0.12 demonstrated.

**α-5 (v0.13 baseline):** Late-target activation operates as v0.13 specifies. Specifically: the end-state cell sampling preserves matched-seed RNG state; activation fires at all-attractors-mastered (with v0.14's amended trigger applying); conditional discovery rate at 160,000 steps approaches the v0.13 batch's 93.9% under v0.14 inheritance, allowing for the v0.14 H2 inversion's effect on activation rates.

**α-6 (v0.14 baseline):** Competency-gated content transformation operates as v0.14 specifies. Specifically: cell-type transitions fire at competency thresholds; the action-selection hard-gate exception admits flagged knowledge cells; three-entry banking on knowledge cells fires; threat-flag clearing co-occurs with knowledge banking; the H2 inversion's direction (v0.14 records higher `attractors_mastered` than the architectural-decomposition prediction) holds.

The Category α verdict is a six-component vector. v1.0 supports the integration claim if all six components pass at the inheritance-distribution-bounded thresholds the prior iteration papers specified. Partial failure on any component is recorded explicitly and analysed before any synthesis is drawn.

### 6.2 Category β (coupling characterisation)

Category β tests whether the heavy-instrumented data confirms the cross-layer coupling identified in v0.14 mechanistically and confirms the phenomenon-class observation across the inheritance chain. Three sub-checks:

**β-1 (mechanism at coupling moments):** At cell-type transition moments, the gate exclusion set updates atomically with the cell-type state change, and the agent's drive-weighted score for the action targeting the transitioned cell shifts in the snapshot triple in a way the obstacle-removal-versus-developmental readings of the v0.14 H2 inversion permit characterisation of. The check passes if the snapshot triples show coherent state transitions at every coupling moment of the relevant class. The check does not adjudicate between the readings — that is reserved for v0.15+ work — but tests whether the instrumented data is sufficient to characterise the readings empirically.

**β-2 (phenomenon-class confirmation at three iterations):** The three documented cross-layer effects — v0.11.2's mastery-affects-threat-side, v0.13's end-state-affects-threat-side, v0.14's bidirectional mastery-and-threat coupling — show, in the instrumented data, the architectural mechanism each iteration's paper claimed. Specifically: the v0.11.2 effect operates through trajectory redistribution (visible in the snapshot data at attractor-banking moments adjacent to hazard cells); the v0.13 effect operates through attentional redirection (visible in the snapshot data at end-state activation moments); the v0.14 effect operates through bidirectional gate-and-mastery coupling (visible in the snapshot data at cell-type transition moments). The check passes if all three effects are mechanistically present in the instrumented data.

**β-3 (escalation to structural property):** Three observations across three iterations is the threshold the v0.14 paper named for confirming the phenomenon-class. v1.0's instrumented data either confirms the observations and supports the escalation from "phenomenon-class confirmed" to "structural property of the architecture" (the language reserved for v1.0 in the v0.14 carry-forward), or shows the observations are present but not at the level of architectural-property generality, in which case the escalation is not made and the framing remains at "phenomenon-class confirmed." This sub-check is the methodologically tightest of the three because the escalation is interpretive; the data either supports it or it does not, and Category β-3 records honestly which.

### 6.3 Category γ (individuation at full-arc scale)

Category γ tests whether the v0.8 individuation finding is preserved at the close of the second arc. The diversity measures Section 5.4 specifies are compared against v0.8's published distributions and against the inheritance-chain CSVs.

**γ-1 (top-attractor diversity preservation):** Distinct top attractors per condition in the v1.0 batch is consistent with v0.8's reported diversity, allowing for the v0.14 H2 inversion's effect on which attractors are reached. The check passes if at least four of the six attractors appear as top attractors across the ten runs per condition in the long-run-length conditions (160,000 steps).

**γ-2 (mastery-sequence diversity preservation):** Distinct mastery sequences per condition is consistent with v0.14's reported diversity. The check passes if mastery-sequence diversity at 160,000 steps is consistent with the 81% divergence-from-v0.13 figure the v0.14 paper reported, indicating the architecture continues to produce biographically distinct trajectories under the full inheritance chain.

**γ-3 (knowledge-banking-order diversity):** Distinct knowledge-banking orders per condition for runs in which all five hazards are banked as knowledge is consistent with the autonomous prioritisation finding the v0.14 paper reported. The check passes if knowledge-banking-order diversity is at or above the level the v0.14 paper's 13% transition-order match figure implies.

The Category γ verdict is a three-component vector. The check supports the foundational v0.8 individuation finding extended across the second arc if all three components pass.

### 6.4 Category δ (negative findings)

Category δ records findings the v1.0 batch produces that constitute architectural properties the integration audit *fails* to demonstrate. The category is pre-registered to honour the methodological discipline that has held throughout the programme: null results, preservation gaps, coupling effects that do not show mechanistically, individuation drift — these are real findings and are reported as such, rather than buried or reframed.

Specific Category δ sub-checks named in advance:

**δ-1 (preservation gap):** Any α-component where preservation fails to the inheritance-distribution-bounded threshold. If α-1 through α-6 do not all pass, the failing component is reported as a δ-1 finding with the specific gap characterised.

**δ-2 (coupling-mechanism gap):** Any β-component where the instrumented data does not confirm the cross-layer effect mechanism the iteration paper claimed. If β-1 or β-2 fails, the failing component is reported as a δ-2 finding, and the architectural-statement paper's claim about the phenomenon-class is correspondingly weakened.

**δ-3 (individuation drift):** Any γ-component where individuation diversity is meaningfully below the inheritance-chain levels. If γ-1, γ-2, or γ-3 fails, the failing component is reported as a δ-3 finding, and the architectural-statement paper's claim about the v0.8 individuation finding's preservation is correspondingly bounded.

**δ-4 (instrumentation artefact):** If the heavy instrumentation produces data the analysis cannot interpret cleanly — snapshot triples that don't capture the coupling moment they were intended to capture, derived metrics that don't separate the mechanisms they were intended to separate — the artefact is reported as a δ-4 finding, and the affected Category β sub-checks are reported as inconclusive rather than as passes or fails.

Category δ is a permissive category: any of the four sub-checks can fire without invalidating the integration audit's overall claim. The audit's overall claim depends on what proportion of α / β / γ pass and what δ findings the failing components produce.

### 6.5 Category Ω (the philosophical claim)

Category Ω names the philosophical claims the architectural-statement paper makes on the basis of what the v1.0 batch shows, and specifies *what kind of evidence the batch provides for each*. The category is pre-registered to make explicit the empirical-versus-interpretive boundary the architectural-statement register requires.

The philosophical claims the v1.0 paper will make:

**Ω-1 (biography is not a function of scale):** The claim that biographical individuation, demonstrated at v0.8 in a small tabular agent and preserved through fourteen iterations of architectural extension, supports the position that biography in artificial learners is a property of architecture rather than of scale or data. The v1.0 batch provides Category γ evidence for this claim. The claim is interpretive in that it generalises beyond the present architecture; the batch establishes that the architectural conditions at this scale produce the signature, not that the architectural conditions at any scale would.

**Ω-2 (development has architectural requirements that can be specified, isolated, and tested):** The claim that the integration audit demonstrates a methodology by which architectural conditions for developmental signatures can be examined systematically — single-variable change per iteration, matched-seed comparison, pre-registration discipline, prospective calibration, temporal-scoping audits, Category F honesty constraints. The v1.0 batch provides Category α + β evidence for this claim. The claim is interpretive in that it generalises from the present programme to the broader question of what governable architectural research looks like; the batch establishes that the methodology operates on this programme, not that it would operate elsewhere.

**Ω-3 (governable AI has methodological prerequisites the present programme has built):** The claim that the methodological apparatus the programme has accumulated — pre-registration, matched-seed audit, calibration discipline, the v0.13.1 temporal-scoping lesson, the v0.14 prospective-calibration lesson, the v1.0 empirical-versus-interpretive distinction — is a contribution to research on governable AI systems independent of the architectural findings the programme has produced. The v1.0 batch provides indirect evidence for this claim through the audit's tractability; the claim does not rest on any specific empirical finding from the batch.

**Ω-4 (the architectural arc is complete on its own terms at v1.0):** The claim that the two arcs (v0.1 → v0.8 establishing developmental signatures, v0.9 → v0.14 building persistent categorical knowledge and demonstrating bidirectional coupling) compose into a coherent architectural statement at v1.0, with what remains being either reserved-for-subsequent-work questions named explicitly in the prior iteration papers or extension-to-richer-environments questions the present architecture's principles bear on without being able to resolve. The v1.0 batch provides Category α + β + γ evidence for this claim collectively; no single sub-check is decisive.

Each Ω claim is bounded honestly. The architectural-statement paper does not claim the v1.0 batch *establishes* any of the four philosophical claims; it claims the batch *supports* them as evidence. The interpretive register the paper holds rests on what the batch shows, but the philosophical register the paper achieves is the paper's, not the batch's.

### 6.6 Category Ω-derived methodological claim about Category F

A note on the relationship between Category F (the iteration papers' honesty constraint) and Category Ω. Category F was the v0.13 paper's mechanism for bounding architectural claims when inherited machinery did the work the iteration appeared to be testing. Category Ω, in v1.0, plays a parallel role at the architectural-statement scale: the philosophical claims the paper makes are bounded by what the batch shows, with the claim and the evidence kept distinct. Category Ω is the architectural-statement-paper analogue of Category F.

This parallel is not stated in the prior iteration papers; it is named here as a methodological observation that arises from the v1.0 audit's design. The architectural-statement paper will treat the parallel explicitly in the methodological-reflection section.

## 7. Methodological commitments

The v1.0 pre-registration is committed to the public repository at github.com/RancidShack/developmental-agent before any v1.0 instrumentation code is written. This commitment is the standing methodological discipline of the programme, applied to v1.0.

The implementation expectation is single-pass without architectural amendment. The instrumentation surface is contained (Section 3.3); the architectural surface is unchanged. The v0.14 codebase, with the recording-layer module added, should produce the v1.0 batch in one pass. Any amendment required is recorded honestly under the amendment policy in Section 8.

The matched-seed comparison apparatus is the seed chain documented in Section 4. No re-run of any prior iteration is performed; the v1.0 batch produces matched-seed comparisons against the existing baseline CSVs.

The prospective-calibration discipline applied prospectively in v0.14 holds in v1.0. Category α thresholds are calibrated against the inheritance distribution: each preserved finding is checked at the level the prior iteration paper reported, allowing for the inheritance-chain effects subsequent papers documented (the v0.14 H2 inversion's effect on activation rates being the most salient).

The empirical-versus-interpretive distinction the Category Ω structure makes explicit is itself a methodological commitment. The architectural-statement paper will hold to the distinction; claims will be marked as empirical or interpretive at the point of statement, not asserted ambiguously.

## 8. Amendment policy

The v1.0 amendment budget is three, consistent with the iteration papers' policy. Amendments are committed to the public repository before any reanalysis of the v1.0 batch they affect is performed. Amendments are scoped to clarification or operationalisation of audit logic rather than architectural change; no amendment may modify the v0.14 architecture v1.0 inherits.

The likely amendment categories, named here in advance:

- **Audit-scope clarification.** If the Category α / β / γ structure surfaces an audit operationalisation issue analogous to the v0.13.1 temporal-scoping lesson, the amendment clarifies the audit logic without modifying the architecture.
- **Threshold calibration adjustment.** If a Category α threshold's prospective calibration turns out to misread the inheritance distribution at v1.0's matched seeds, the amendment recalibrates honestly with the methodological reasoning recorded.
- **Snapshot-content adjustment.** If the heavy instrumentation specification produces data that does not support the Category β derived metrics as the pre-reg specifies, the amendment adjusts the snapshot content with the analytical implication recorded. This amendment category is the most likely to fire and is named explicitly here.

Amendments outside these categories require explicit justification and are recorded with the same honesty as the v0.13.1 amendment.

## 9. The empirical-versus-interpretive boundary

This section names what v1.0 is testing and what v1.0 is not testing, to bound the architectural-statement paper's claims in advance.

**What v1.0 is testing:**

- Whether the cumulative inheritance chain preserves the headline findings of each iteration at matched seeds (Category α).
- Whether the cross-layer coupling identified in v0.14 operates mechanistically at the level the heavy instrumentation captures (Category β).
- Whether the v0.8 individuation finding is preserved at the close of the second arc (Category γ).
- Whether any preservation gaps, coupling-mechanism gaps, individuation drift, or instrumentation artefacts surface (Category δ).

**What v1.0 is not testing:**

- Whether the architectural principles generalise beyond the present tabular environment to richer environments. This is the v0.8 essay's third extension and the present paper's forward-looking question; v1.0 names it but does not test it.
- Whether the H2 inversion's two readings (obstacle-removal mechanistic versus developmental) can be distinguished. This is reserved for v0.15 obstacle-removal-isolation work; v1.0 captures the data that the readings would predict but does not adjudicate between them.
- Whether the architectural principles generalise to non-tabular representations. This is the v0.8 essay's third extension, still reserved.
- Whether the architectural principles generalise to multi-agent settings, robotic-enhanced environment awareness, or other extensions the integration paper names as forward-looking questions.

**What the architectural-statement paper claims on the basis of what v1.0 tests:**

The four Ω claims. Each is bounded by the evidence the relevant α / β / γ sub-checks provide. The paper's philosophical register depends on the empirical evidence supporting it; the paper does not run the philosophical register past what the evidence carries.

The empirical-versus-interpretive boundary is the most important methodological commitment in v1.0's pre-registration. The architectural-statement register the paper holds is achievable only because the boundary is held cleanly. Where the boundary blurs, the paper's claims weaken correspondingly. Pre-registering this in advance — rather than asserting it post-hoc as a self-evaluation — is the methodological commitment that distinguishes architectural-statement work from architectural-rhetoric work.
