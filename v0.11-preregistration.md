# v0.11 Pre-Registration: Experience-Driven Attractor Depletion and Persistent Mastery Representation

**Author:** Nicholas P M Baker, Synapstak Ltd
**Date:** 24 April 2026
**Status:** Pre-registration, committed before any v0.11 code is written
**Preceding work:** v0.10 (Baker, 2026d), v0.9 (Baker, 2026c), v0.8 (Baker, 2026a)

---

## 1. Purpose

v0.10 (Baker, 2026d) demonstrated that an experience-driven persistent threat layer resolves the long-horizon hazard-avoidance instability identified in v0.9. At 160,000 steps, v0.10 produced 60/60 Phase 3 cleanness across all cost levels, a 20-percentage-point improvement over v0.9's 48/60. The threat layer also reduced total cost incurred by 50-75% across cost levels, demonstrating that persistent representation makes learned avoidance both more stable and more efficient.

v0.10's success established the architectural principle that repeated experience can be converted into categorical persistent knowledge through an N-threshold counter-and-flag mechanism operating outside the Q-value system. The principle resolves the v0.9 instability for hazards specifically: bounded learned knowledge, stored distributedly in Q-values, had been overwhelmed by unbounded preference accumulation at extended horizons. The threat layer addressed this by moving categorical hazard knowledge into a persistent representation that does not compete with preferences on the same axis.

The same architectural question applies to positive experience. The v0.9/v0.10 preference accumulator is unbounded: cell_preference grows monotonically with visit frequency and has no mechanism that caps, normalises, or terminates the accumulation. Across the research programme to date, this has been architecturally useful — it is what produces the strong Phase 3 settling behaviour and the feature-alignment finding. But it also produces behaviour that does not match a key developmental signature: a learner that has mastered what one region of its world offers should be drawn toward unmastered regions, not continue to accumulate unbounded preference for what it already knows.

v0.11 tests whether applying the same architectural pattern used for hazards in v0.10 — repeated experience converting to categorical persistent knowledge, stored outside the accumulator that dominates action selection — produces a bounded mastery mechanism that changes the developmental trajectory from "find and settle" to "systematically master and move on."

## 2. Central Hypothesis

Adding an experience-driven attractor depletion mechanism and a persistent mastery layer to the v0.10 architecture will produce a systematically different Phase 3 developmental trajectory. Specifically, agents in v0.11 at extended run lengths will master multiple attractors rather than settling on a single attractor, with Phase 3 visit distribution shifting from high concentration on one cell to distributed visits across unmastered attractors.

## 3. Secondary Hypotheses

The broader developmental signatures established in v0.8 and held through v0.9 and v0.10 — Phase 3 concentration, biographical individuation, rule adherence (as maintained by the v0.10 threat layer), and mastery count — will be preserved but will manifest differently.

Concentration is predicted to decrease: a v0.11 agent visiting multiple attractors serially will show lower Phase 3 concentration on any single cell than v0.10 agents settling on one favourite. This is not a failure mode; it is the predicted behavioural consequence of the new architecture.

Individuation is predicted to be preserved but will manifest as variation in the *order* of mastery rather than variation in settled destination. Different agents will master the six attractors in different sequences, and the sequence itself becomes the biographical signature.

Rule adherence (hazard avoidance) inherited from v0.10 is expected to remain at full stabilisation at extended run lengths.

## 4. Architectural Specification

v0.11 inherits v0.10 unchanged except for the modifications specified in this section.

### 4.1 Base configuration

v0.11 operates with the v0.10 architecture as its starting point:

- FRAME cells: impassable, pre-wired aversion retained, epsilon filter active.
- HAZARD cells: passable at scalar cost, no pre-wired aversion, threat layer active (experience-driven flagging at N=3 entries).
- ATTRACTOR cells: passable, feature reward on entry — **modified in v0.11 as specified below**.
- NEUTRAL cells: passable, no reward of their own.

All v0.10 architectural elements — three-phase developmental schedule, drive composition, learning rate, discount factor, epsilon, Q-value reset multipliers, preference accumulation, forward model, prediction error windows, threat layer and hazard-entry counter — are retained unchanged.

### 4.2 Attractor depletion mechanism

A new per-cell counter is added: `attractor_visit_counter`, a dict indexed by cell coordinates, initialised to zero for all ATTRACTOR cells at agent creation.

Each time the agent enters an ATTRACTOR cell, the counter for that cell increments by 1, across all phases.

The attractor's feature reward behaviour is modified as follows:

- If `attractor_visit_counter[cell] < MASTERY_THRESHOLD`: the agent receives the standard feature reward (0.15 units, unchanged from v0.8-v0.10) on entry.
- If `attractor_visit_counter[cell] == MASTERY_THRESHOLD`: the attractor is banked (see 4.3 below), and a one-time mastery bonus is delivered in place of the standard feature reward on this entry.
- If `attractor_visit_counter[cell] > MASTERY_THRESHOLD`: the agent receives zero feature reward on entry. The attractor is depleted.

### 4.3 Persistent mastery layer and mastery bonus

A new architectural component is added: a per-cell mastery flag, binary-valued, stored as a structure indexed by cell coordinates. At agent creation:

- All ATTRACTOR cells are unflagged in the mastery layer (`mastery_flag[cell] = 0`).
- FRAME, HAZARD, and NEUTRAL cells do not have entries in the mastery layer.

When `attractor_visit_counter[cell]` reaches `MASTERY_THRESHOLD` on entry:

- `mastery_flag[cell]` is set to 1 (one-way; does not clear).
- A one-time `MASTERY_BONUS` is delivered to the agent's intrinsic reward stream at that step, replacing the standard feature reward.
- The cell's coordinates are recorded in a mastered-attractors set for analysis.

### 4.4 Parameter specification

Three parameters are pre-registered:

- **MASTERY_THRESHOLD = 3**: Matches v0.10's flag threshold for architectural parallelism. Three visits before depletion and banking. Not varied within this experiment.

- **MASTERY_BONUS = 1.0**: The one-time bonus delivered at the moment of banking. This value is substantially larger than a single feature reward (0.15) but substantially smaller than the cumulative feature reward the attractor would have delivered had it not depleted (0.15 × 3 = 0.45 plus the 1.0 bonus = 1.45 total feature reward across the attractor's life, compared to unbounded in v0.10). The design intent is that banking produces a noticeable signal without dominating the per-visit feature reward during the approach-and-master phase.

- **PREFERENCE_ACCUMULATES_BONUS = True**: The one-time mastery bonus is added to the cell_preference accumulator at the banking step, alongside the standard preference update. This maintains the v0.9/v0.10 architectural pattern where preference accumulates from both learning-progress and feature rewards. The bonus itself flows through preference accumulation normally.

### 4.5 Interaction with existing v0.10 mechanisms

The attractor depletion mechanism interacts with several existing architectural elements. The interactions are specified here to avoid ambiguity.

**Primitive attraction bias.** v0.8's primitive `ATTRACTION_BONUS` of +0.3 on adjacent attractor cells is retained unchanged in v0.11. This bias is pre-wired and perceptual; it does not depend on mastery status. An attractor that has been mastered (feature reward depleted to zero) still produces the attraction bias on adjacent cells during action selection. This is a deliberate design choice: the primitive perceptual bias represents the agent's pre-wired orientation toward marked features of the world, not its learned evaluation of them. The mastery mechanism operates on reward, not on perception.

**Preference accumulation.** cell_preference continues to accumulate on every visit to any cell, unchanged from v0.10. For ATTRACTOR cells pre-depletion, preference accumulates feature reward (0.15) plus any learning-progress reward. At the banking step, preference accumulates the MASTERY_BONUS (1.0) plus any learning-progress reward. Post-depletion, preference accumulates only learning-progress reward (feature reward is zero). This means mastered attractors continue to have accumulated preference mass, but the preference stops growing as fast as it would have under the v0.9/v0.10 unbounded mechanism.

**Threat layer.** The v0.10 threat layer is retained without modification. Hazard avoidance operates as in v0.10. The mastery mechanism applies only to ATTRACTOR cells.

**Q-value learning.** Standard TD learning operates on the combined intrinsic reward signal, which now includes the MASTERY_BONUS at banking steps and zero feature reward at post-depletion entries. Q-values will reflect these changes naturally through the learning update; no separate mechanism is needed.

### 4.6 What v0.11 does not change

- Hazard handling: inherited from v0.10 unchanged. Threat layer, N=3 conversion, hard gate, FRAME pre-flagging.
- Cost mechanism: unchanged. Hazard cost applies as in v0.10.
- Phase schedule: unchanged. Three phases, prescribed acquisition, integration, autonomy.
- Drive composition: unchanged. Novelty, learning progress, preference, feature weights identical to v0.10.
- Learning parameters: unchanged. Learning rate 0.1, discount 0.9, epsilon 0.1.
- Primitive biases: FRAME aversion and ATTRACTOR attraction retained.
- Environment layout: same 20×20 grid, same six attractors, same five hazards, same start cell.

### 4.7 Per-cell mastery, no category-level generalisation

As with v0.10's threat layer, the mastery mechanism operates per-cell. There is no category-level generalisation: mastering one attractor has no effect on the counters or flags of other attractors. The agent must encounter each attractor and visit it MASTERY_THRESHOLD times before that specific cell is banked.

This is a deliberate design choice matching v0.10's per-cell-no-generalisation property. Category-level mastery — where the agent generalises from "this attractor at (3, 3) is mastered" to "attractors of this type are mastered" — would require a perceptual signature distinguishing different kinds of attractor, which the current environment does not provide. All six attractors are identical in type and differ only in position. Extension to differentiated attractor types is flagged as a separate research question for later work.

Variation in mastery order across runs is therefore expected and reflects the agent's specific exploration trajectory. Some agents will master certain attractors early in Phase 2 and others later; the order of mastery is itself the biographical signature this iteration investigates.

## 5. Experimental Design

### 5.1 Matched comparison

v0.11 is compared against v0.10 at matched conditions and matched seeds. This produces within-seed behavioural comparison up to the point at which depletion first occurs.

### 5.2 Experimental matrix

| Variable | Values |
|----------|--------|
| Architecture | v0.10 (as published in v0.10 batch), v0.11 (depletion + mastery layer) |
| Cost level | 0.1, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 5.0, 10.0 |
| Run length (steps) | 20,000, 80,000, 160,000 |
| Runs per cell | 10 |

Total runs: 2 architectures × 6 cost levels × 3 run lengths × 10 runs = 360 runs.

Cost levels are retained across both architectures despite cost being a hazard-side parameter, to ensure exact matched-seed comparison. v0.11's behaviour should be robust across cost levels because the depletion mechanism does not depend on hazard cost; including this variation provides confirmation that v0.11 does not exhibit unexpected cost-sensitivity on the attractor side.

### 5.3 Computational estimate

Based on v0.10's observed compute (7.0 minutes for 360 runs), the v0.11 matrix is estimated at 8-12 minutes total. The additional bookkeeping (attractor visit counter, mastery flag check at each attractor entry) is minimal and should not significantly affect runtime.

## 6. Metrics

### 6.1 Metrics retained from v0.8, v0.9, and v0.10

All metrics from preceding work are retained unchanged:

- Hazard entries by phase (P1, P2, P3, total)
- Phase 3 concentration
- Phase 3 attractor ratio
- Mastery count (state-action pairs meeting the mastery criterion from v0.8)
- Top-5 preferences on attractors and near attractors
- Individuation (distinct top attractors across batch)
- Rule violations (Phase 3 and full-run cleanness)
- Threat-layer metrics from v0.10: hazards flagged, time-to-first-flag, time-to-final-flag, cost-at-final-flag, actions gated by threat layer

### 6.2 New metrics specific to v0.11

- **Attractors mastered per run.** Count of ATTRACTOR cells with mastery_flag = 1 at end of run. Primary measure for v0.11's central hypothesis. Expected values: in v0.11, increasing with run length and potentially reaching 6/6 at long run lengths; in v0.10 comparison, undefined (no mastery mechanism).

- **Time-to-first-mastery.** Step at which the first attractor was banked. Analogous to v0.10's time-to-first-flag. Measures how quickly the mastery mechanism begins operating.

- **Time-to-final-mastery.** Step at which the last attractor banked during the run was banked. For runs that master all six, this is the point at which developmental mastery is complete.

- **Mastery order sequence.** The ordered sequence of attractor coordinates in the order they were banked. Preserves the biographical signature of each agent's developmental trajectory.

- **Distinct mastery orders across batch.** Count of distinct first-banked attractors, second-banked attractors, etc. across runs. A full-individuation signal would show all six attractors appearing as the first banked in at least one run across the batch.

- **Preference mass at depletion.** The accumulated preference value on each attractor at the moment of banking. Allows post-hoc analysis of whether mastered attractors still dominate preferences post-banking.

- **Phase 3 visit distribution across mastered vs unmastered attractors.** Measures whether Phase 3 visits shift toward unmastered attractors. Directly relevant to the central hypothesis.

### 6.3 Expected reporting format

Per-cost-level central tendencies and distribution summaries in prose, matching v0.8 through v0.10 convention. Per-run numerical data preserved in CSV files.

## 7. Pre-Registered Interpretation Categories

Category assignment is based on the primary measures: attractors mastered per run, and Phase 3 visit distribution across mastered versus unmastered attractors, at extended run lengths (80,000 and 160,000 steps).

### Category A — Full mastery trajectory

v0.11 at 160,000 steps produces a mean of ≥5.0 attractors mastered per run, with Phase 3 visits distributed broadly across unmastered attractors where any remain. The central hypothesis is strongly supported: the architecture produces the systematically-working-through-attractors trajectory predicted.

### Category B — Partial mastery trajectory

v0.11 at 160,000 steps produces a mean of 3.0-4.9 attractors mastered per run. The mechanism operates but does not reach full mastery across the batch. Secondary analysis would examine whether mastery count correlates with cost level, run length within the tested range, or other measured properties.

### Category C — Weak or incomplete mastery

v0.11 at 160,000 steps produces a mean of <3.0 attractors mastered per run. The mechanism as specified does not produce the predicted behavioural change. Would motivate investigation of whether the MASTERY_BONUS is too small to drive sufficient attractor-seeking, whether the depletion is too aggressive, or whether some other architectural interaction prevents the predicted dynamic from emerging.

### Category D — Over-mastery

v0.11 produces full 6/6 mastery at 20,000 steps across most runs — i.e., the mechanism operates so aggressively that mastery completes well before Phase 3 autonomy begins. Would suggest MASTERY_THRESHOLD should be higher or that the attractor-seeking dynamic produced by depletion is too strong.

### Category E — Developmental degradation

The developmental signatures preserved through v0.9 and v0.10 (rule adherence, individuation, mastery count, attractor ratio during Phase 2) are substantially reduced in v0.11 even if the mastery dynamics work as predicted. This would indicate the depletion mechanism disrupts broader developmental behaviours despite the narrower prediction being confirmed. Would motivate rethinking the interaction between depletion and preference accumulation.

### Category F — Individuation collapse

v0.11 produces high mastery counts but the order of mastery is highly convergent across runs — most agents master the same attractors in the same order. Would indicate that the depletion mechanism produces deterministic exploration patterns that undermine the individuation signature that has held throughout the programme.

## 8. Commitments

Three methodological commitments accompany this pre-registration, matching the discipline of v0.9 and v0.10:

First, no v0.11 code will be written before this pre-registration document is committed to the public repository.

Second, the experimental matrix and metric definitions will not be revised during or after batch execution. Any revision necessitated by implementation-time discovery will be handled through an explicit amendment document, committed before the amended computation is run.

Third, findings will be reported as they occur, including findings that fall between categories, indicate unanticipated patterns, or contradict the central hypothesis.

## 9. Relationship to v0.10 and v0.12

v0.11 completes the architectural programme of converting unbounded accumulation into bounded categorical knowledge through persistent representation. v0.10 addressed this for the hazard side; v0.11 addresses it for the mastery side. Together they produce an architecture with bounded learning for both valences of experience, with persistent storage for categorical knowledge that resists erosion by ongoing dynamics.

v0.12 is reserved for a separate architectural question: category-level generalisation for hazards, addressing the late-encountered-hazard finding identified in the v0.10 outlier analysis. v0.12 will introduce signature-matching that allows a hazard cell whose perceptual signature matches known flagged hazards to be flagged on first entry rather than after N entries, alongside a bonus for successful avoidance that converts gated avoidance into rewarded avoidance. v0.12 is architecturally independent of v0.11; the two iterations could in principle be implemented in either order.

v0.13 and beyond are reserved for more substantial architectural questions including differentiated attractor types with category-level preference for mastery diversity, and other questions the completed v0.8-v0.12 architecture makes tractable.
