**Date:** 25 April 2026
**Status:** Pre-registration, committed before any v0.13 code is written
**Inheritance baseline:** v0.12 (Baker, 2026h)
**Preceding work:** v0.12 pre-registration (Baker, 2026h), v0.12 paper (Baker, 2026i, in preparation), v0.11.2 amendment (Baker, 2026g), v0.11.1 amendment (Baker, 2026f), v0.11 pre-registration (Baker, 2026e), v0.10 (Baker, 2026d)

## 1. Reason for iteration

The v0.11.2 batch (Baker, 2026g) reported a convergence finding: at 160,000 steps, mean attractors mastered approached 5.87 of 6 across the batch, with 53 of 60 runs reaching full mastery. The v0.12 batch (Baker, 2026i, in preparation) confirmed and refined this: 49 of 60 runs reached full mastery at 160,000 steps under v0.12's signature-matching threat-layer extension, with mean attractors mastered at 5.80 of 6 and the v0.11.2 individuation Category F not triggered on any of its three signals. The architecture, by 160,000 steps under v0.12 inheritance, reliably converges toward exhausting the available attractor set.

This convergence opens a question the architecture has not previously been asked. When the agent has banked all available attractors — when the four mastery interventions developed across the v0.11 series have depleted feature reward, cleared attraction bias, reset preference, and blocked accumulation across all six attractor cells — the agent's behavioural environment becomes what v0.12's batch has documented but the programme has not yet examined: a 20×20 grid in which the agent has found everything there is to find. The v0.11.2 meta-report at 160,000 steps shows distinct top-attractor count of 1 of 6 and top attractor distribution of (3, 3) at 60 of 60 runs. v0.12 produces an identical pattern at 160,000 steps. This is not concentration through mastery accumulation — both v0.11.2 and v0.12 have explicitly bounded such accumulation. It is concentration through structural exhaustion: with all attractors banked, (3, 3) is the closest banked cell to the agent's start position at (1, 1), and post-mastery exploration ranges around it because nothing else in the environment produces attractor-side reward.

The architectural question this opens, framed as the v0.13 iteration, is whether the architecture can be given something *new* to find at the moment the existing attractor set is exhausted, and whether the architecture's locating machinery handles this new target reliably. The Montessori-analogy framing identified in the carry-forward record names the question this way: the child who has worked through the prepared environment to its limits, and has banked what it offers, is presumably ready for the next thing. The architectural test is whether something that appears at this moment — at the moment of all-attractors-mastered — can be located by the agent, given that the attractor-side reward sources that drove Phase 2 attention have been depleted.

The iteration is the Montessori-analogy work the carry-forward record reserved for v0.13, scoped narrowly. v0.13 establishes the locating mechanism in isolation. What the located cell is for — payout structure, transformation into a knowledge cell, run termination, or any subsequent behavioural consequence — is reserved for v0.14 or v0.15, on the design framework that emerged from the v0.12 design conversation about competency-gated content. v0.13 commits only to: a new cell appears at a random location at the moment of all-attractors-mastered, and the agent locates it.

The decision to do v0.12 first, with v0.13 following, was deliberate (Baker, 2026h, Section 1). v0.12's signature-matching addressed the older v0.10 outlier finding while v0.13 had fresher v0.11.2 momentum behind it; either order was defensible, and v0.12 was chosen on the methodological-arc grounds that the threat-layer programme had a coherent v0.9 → v0.10 → v0.12 sequence. v0.13 is now the natural successor: v0.11.2's convergence finding is its evidence base, v0.12's signature-matching is preserved without modification, and the v0.13 question can be posed cleanly because all prior iterations have closed the gaps that would otherwise have confounded it.

## 2. The architectural change

The v0.13 architecture inherits v0.12 unchanged except for the addition of one new cell type, one new activation signal, and one modification to the agent's perceptual handling of cell-type transitions during a run.

### 2.1 New cell type

A new cell type, `END_STATE`, is added to the environment's type system. The constant `END_STATE = 4` is added to the cell-type constants alongside `FRAME`, `NEUTRAL`, `HAZARD`, `ATTRACTOR`. Cells of type `END_STATE` are passable, carry the same feature reward and primitive attraction bias as `ATTRACTOR` cells (FEATURE_DRIVE_WEIGHT = 0.15 per visit, ATTRACTION_BONUS = 0.3 in the primitive-bias pipeline), and are subject to a banking rule specific to v0.13.

### 2.2 End-state cell location

At run start, the environment selects one neutral cell coordinate at random from `world.scope_cells \ world.attractor_cells` and designates it as the end-state cell location. The selection uses the agent's NumPy random state, so it is deterministic with respect to the run's seed and matches across the matched-seed comparison with v0.12.

The selected coordinate is recorded as `world.end_state_cell` for metric tracking but its cell type remains `NEUTRAL` for the duration of the pre-activation period. The agent perceives it as a neutral cell, visits it during Phase 1's prescribed traversal as it would visit any neutral cell, and may visit it incidentally during Phase 2 and Phase 3 without any architectural consequence. The cell carries no feature reward and no attraction bias during the pre-activation period.

### 2.3 Activation signal

After every step, the agent checks whether `sum(self.mastery_flag.values()) == len(self.world.attractor_cells)` — that is, whether all six attractor cells have been banked. The check is performed in the agent's main loop, after `update_model` and `update_values` have completed for the current step.

When the check first becomes true (the all-attractors-mastered signal fires), the agent records the step at which activation occurred (`self.activation_step = step`) and triggers the cell type transition specified in Section 2.4. Subsequent checks within the same run do not retrigger activation; activation is one-way per run.

### 2.4 Cell type transition on activation

At the activation step, the environment's `cell_type` dictionary is updated for the end-state cell coordinate: `self.cell_type[self.end_state_cell] = END_STATE`. The cell's perceptual properties change immediately. From the next action selection onward, the agent's `perceive_adjacent` returns `END_STATE` for adjacent cells at the end-state location, and the action-selection pipeline computes feature reward and primitive attraction bias for the end-state cell as it does for attractor cells.

### 2.5 Banking rule for end-state cell

Unlike attractor cells, which bank after MASTERY_THRESHOLD = 3 entries, the end-state cell banks on first entry after activation. The architectural reasoning: the cell is found, not mastered. v0.13's research question is whether the agent locates the cell, not whether the agent learns about the cell across multiple visits.

When the agent enters the end-state cell for the first time after activation, the architecture records the step (`self.end_state_found_step = step`), increments a separate end-state visit counter (`self.end_state_visits` for use in any further v0.13 metric tracking), and applies the four mastery interventions to the end-state cell that v0.11.2 applies to mastered attractors: feature reward depletes to zero, primitive attraction bias clears, cell preference is reset to zero at this step, and ongoing preference accumulation on the cell is blocked.

After the end-state cell is banked, the agent has no remaining feature-reward source in the environment. Phase 3 dynamics continue without modification — novelty drive, preference accumulation on non-attractor cells, learning progress, threat-layer hard gate, and v0.12 signature-matching all operate as in v0.12. The run continues until the deterministic step-budget completes.

### 2.6 Implementation surface

The v0.13 architectural change is contained to a single research question — does the architecture support late-appearing target location through the existing attractor-finding machinery — but the implementation surface is larger than v0.12's was. v0.12 changed one rule in one method (`update_threat_layer`). v0.13 modifies:

- `StructuredGridWorld.__init__` to sample and record the end-state cell coordinate
- `StructuredGridWorld.cell_type` to support mid-run cell type transitions
- The cell-type constant set to add `END_STATE = 4`
- The agent's main run loop to check the activation signal and trigger the cell type transition
- The agent's banking logic to handle the end-state cell's one-entry banking parallel to the four mastery interventions
- The metric set to record `activation_step`, `end_state_cell` location, and `end_state_found_step`

This is named honestly in this pre-registration so that the methodological record reflects the difference. v0.12 was a one-rule iteration. v0.13 is a one-architectural-goal iteration whose implementation requires coordinated changes across world, agent, and metric systems. The single-variable-change discipline holds at the research-question level. Whether the implementation completes in a single pass without amendment is the empirical question this pre-registration's commitments are designed to test, and Section 10 names the stopping rule that applies if amendment becomes necessary.

## 3. What v0.13 does not change

All architectural elements from v0.12 are preserved unchanged.

The threat layer operates as v0.12 specifies. Frame cells are pre-flagged at agent creation. Hazard cells follow the v0.12 signature-matching flag-conversion rule: the first hazard cell encountered requires three entries; every subsequent hazard cell encountered after at least one is flagged converts on first entry. The hard-gate action-selection rule remains: any action targeting a flagged cell is excluded from the action-selection pipeline before Q-value computation, primitive bias computation, or epsilon-greedy random selection.

The mastery layer operates as v0.11.2 specifies. The four mastery interventions hold for attractor cells: zero feature reward post-mastery, cleared attraction bias for mastered cells, preference reset at banking, blocked preference accumulation post-banking. MASTERY_THRESHOLD = 3 and MASTERY_BONUS = 1.0 are inherited unchanged.

The three-phase developmental schedule, drive composition at each phase, learning rate 0.1, discount factor 0.9, and epsilon 0.1 are inherited unchanged. The pre-wired aversion bias of −5.0 for frame-adjacent actions is retained; hazards have no pre-wired aversion. The agent starts each run at coordinate (1, 1).

The single-variable-change discipline holds at the research-question level: v0.13 introduces end-state target activation as the singular architectural extension. All prior architectural elements are preserved.

## 4. Predicted behavioural change

Under v0.13, the predicted developmental trajectory is the v0.12 trajectory through Phase 1 and Phase 2 and through the all-attractors-mastered moment, followed by a v0.13-specific post-activation trajectory in which the agent's exploration is partially redirected toward the new feature-reward source. The agent's locating of the end-state cell is the behavioural finding the iteration tests.

The mechanistic prediction has three components.

First, the activation step distribution under v0.13 should match the time-to-final-mastery distribution observed in the v0.12 batch under matched seeds, since the activation signal is identical to v0.12's final-mastery condition. Mean activation steps should approximate 8,333 at 20,000-step runs, 23,599 at 80,000, and 36,839 at 160,000. Variance across runs is substantial — at 20,000 steps, time-to-final-mastery ranges from 638 to 19,783 in the v0.12 batch — and this variance carries forward to the post-activation window distribution.

Second, the post-activation window — the number of steps remaining after activation — varies systematically across runs and run lengths. At 160,000 steps, the mean post-activation window is approximately 123,000 steps with a minimum of approximately 53,000. At 80,000 steps, mean approximately 56,000 with minimum approximately 25,000. At 20,000 steps, mean approximately 11,667 with minimum approximately 217. Some 20,000-step runs will activate too late for any locating mechanism to succeed within the remaining budget. This is named explicitly here as a pre-registered expectation, not as a confound.

Third, the agent's locating behaviour in the post-activation window is predicted to operate through the existing attractor-finding machinery, re-engaged on the new feature-reward source. The four mastery interventions have depleted attractor-side reward signals; the end-state cell, on activation, is the only cell in the environment producing feature reward. The agent's novelty drive, learning-progress drive, and primitive-attraction-bias mechanism — which have been bounded by the v0.11.2 interventions for mastered attractors — should respond to the end-state cell as they would respond to any new feature-rewarding cell. The agent locates the cell through the same mechanism that locates attractors during Phase 2.

## 5. Experimental matrix

The experimental matrix is one architecture (v0.13) crossed with six cost levels (0.1, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 5.0, 10.0) crossed with three run lengths (20,000, 80,000, 160,000 steps) crossed with ten runs per cell, totalling 180 runs.

For each (cost, run length, run index) triple, the random seed is matched to the v0.11.2 batch (Baker, 2026g) at the same condition. Seeds are loaded from `run_data_v0_11_2.csv` rather than regenerated, on the technical reasoning named in v0.12's pre-registration Section 5 (Python's hash randomisation per session prevents deterministic seed regeneration; the recorded seeds in the CSV are the data of record).

The matched-seed comparison is across three architectures simultaneously: v0.10, v0.11.2, and v0.12 (all from existing batches in `run_data_v0_11_2.csv` and `run_data_v0_12.csv`) provide the comparison baselines for any v0.13 finding that touches their inherited dynamics. v0.13's behavioural-preservation audit is performed against v0.12 specifically, since v0.12 is the inheritance baseline.

Cost levels and run lengths are inherited from the threat-layer programme to maintain methodological symmetry. The cost variable affects the rate at which hazards are encountered and converted in v0.10 / v0.11.2 / v0.12 but does not directly affect mastery dynamics or the activation signal in v0.13. It is retained to enable comparison across the full programme matrix and to test whether v0.13's locating mechanism interacts with cost in any unanticipated way.

## 6. Metrics

All v0.10 metrics, all v0.11.2 metrics, and all v0.12 metrics are retained.

Three new metrics are introduced for v0.13.

**Activation step.** The step at which the all-attractors-mastered signal first fires for a run. Records when the agent has banked all six attractors and the end-state cell becomes active. Null for runs that do not reach all-attractors-mastered within the run length. Distribution of activation steps across the batch is reported as a finding.

**End-state cell location.** The coordinate of the end-state cell, sampled at run start from `scope_cells \ attractor_cells`. Recorded for the metric set to enable per-run analysis of locating-mechanism performance as a function of cell location, distance from the agent's start position, and adjacency to other landmarks. Distribution of end-state cell locations across the batch is reported as a finding.

**End-state found step.** The step at which the agent first enters the end-state cell after activation. Null if the agent does not enter the cell within the run length. The post-activation discovery time, defined as `end_state_found_step - activation_step`, is the diagnostic metric for the locating mechanism's efficiency. Distribution of post-activation discovery times across the batch, conditional on activation having occurred, is the headline finding for v0.13's mechanistic claim.

The headline finding for v0.13's operational claim is the **discovery rate**: the fraction of runs in which the agent enters the end-state cell at least once after activation. Discovery rate is reported per run length, per cost level, and as a total across the batch.

## 7. Pre-registered interpretation categories

Six interpretation categories are pre-registered. Categories A through E follow the v0.10 / v0.11.2 / v0.12 pattern. Category F is the v0.13-specific honesty constraint named in Section 8.

**Category A — successful late-target location with full preservation.** v0.13 produces all four of the following:

- Discovery rate ≥ 30 of 60 at 20,000 steps across all six cost levels. The 30 of 60 threshold acknowledges that some 20,000-step runs activate too late for any locating mechanism to succeed within the remaining budget; it is calibrated to the post-activation window distribution that 20,000-step runs produce.
- Discovery rate ≥ 50 of 60 at 80,000 steps across all six cost levels. The 50 of 60 threshold accommodates some failures at the high-cost end where time-to-final-mastery extends; it tests whether the locating mechanism succeeds reliably when the post-activation window is in the tens-of-thousands of steps.
- Discovery rate ≥ 55 of 60 at 160,000 steps across all six cost levels. The 55 of 60 threshold parallels v0.10 and v0.12 Category A thresholds at 160,000 steps; it tests whether the locating mechanism operates reliably when the post-activation window is approximately 123,000 steps on average.
- Behavioural preservation: pre-activation behaviour byte-identical to v0.12 at matched seeds across all 180 runs. Specifically: hazard entries by phase, hazards flagged, time to first flag, time to second flag, time to final flag, first-entry conversions, attractors mastered, time to first mastery, time to final mastery, mastery sequence, and first-banked attractor identity must all match v0.12's recorded values for the same (cost, run length, run index) triple, up to the activation step.

Category A is the pre-registered success condition. It requires v0.13 to demonstrate the locating mechanism operates across the run-length spectrum, with thresholds calibrated to the post-activation window distributions, while preserving v0.12's pre-activation behaviour exactly.

**Category B — partial location.** v0.13 produces at least one Category A discovery-rate threshold but not all three, with the behavioural-preservation audit otherwise satisfied.

**Category C — locating mechanism fails.** v0.13 produces discovery rates substantially below the Category A thresholds across two or more run lengths. The architectural hypothesis — that the existing attractor-finding machinery re-engages on the new feature-reward source — is not supported by the data.

**Category D — over-activation.** Any of the following:

- The end-state cell becomes perceptible-as-end-state at any step before activation
- The activation signal fires before all six attractors are banked
- The end-state cell is selected at a coordinate not in `scope_cells \ attractor_cells`
- The agent enters the end-state cell pre-activation and the cell banks, treating pre-activation visits as discoveries

**Category E — developmental degradation.** Pre-activation behaviour fails the byte-identical preservation audit at the matched-seed level. v0.10, v0.11.2, or v0.12 signatures are disturbed in the pre-activation period. This category triggers if any pre-activation metric differs from v0.12's recorded value at the same (cost, run length, run index) triple.

**Category F — interpretation collapse: locating capability attributable to inherited dynamics.** v0.13 produces Category A or Category B at the discovery-rate level, but the mechanistic interpretation is bounded by the architectural fact that the locating capability operates through inherited Phase 3 dynamics rather than through any v0.13-specific addition. The agent's novelty drive, learning-progress drive, primitive-attraction-bias mechanism, and preference accumulation machinery are all v0.11.2 inheritance. v0.13 contributes the cell type, the activation signal, and the cell-type transition; the locating itself is performed by machinery the architecture already had.

This is not a failure condition in the operational sense — the discovery rate may meet Category A thresholds and the preservation audit may pass. Category F is a pre-registered honesty constraint on the architectural claim. If Category F applies, the v0.13 paper will report the operational success while explicitly bounding the architectural claim to "the v0.11.2 / v0.12 inherited locating machinery handles late-appearing targets at random locations" rather than "v0.13 introduces a locating capability."

The Category F constraint applies to any v0.13 result in which the agent locates the end-state cell at meaningful rates. The pre-registration commits in advance to handling the architectural claim accordingly. The richer-mechanism work (a locating mechanism specific to end-state cells, beacon-style perceptual signals, learned end-state-detection rather than rule-based detection) is reserved for v0.15 or later if the question becomes tractable.

## 8. Predicted result and the architectural claim being made

The pre-registered prediction is Category A.

The mechanistic prediction is that v0.13 agents reach all-attractors-mastered at the same steps v0.12 agents do (since the activation signal is the all-attractors-mastered condition that v0.12's batch records), then redirect Phase 3 attention toward the end-state cell as the only remaining feature-rewarding cell in the environment. The novelty drive, attenuated for known cells and elevated for novel cell-type-transitions, contributes to the pull. The primitive attraction bias on cells adjacent to the end-state cell contributes to the pull. The preference accumulation on the end-state cell, beginning at zero at activation and accumulating on subsequent visits, contributes to sustained engagement once the cell is found. The agent enters the end-state cell within the post-activation window in most runs at 80,000 and 160,000 steps and in a meaningful subset of runs at 20,000 steps.

The behavioural-preservation prediction is that v0.13's pre-activation behaviour is byte-identical to v0.12 at matched seeds. The architectural change — the end-state cell exists as a neutral cell pre-activation — does not affect any aspect of pre-activation perception, action selection, or learning dynamics. The agent at any pre-activation step is operating in an environment that is, from its perspective, identical to v0.12's environment. Byte-identical pre-activation behaviour is therefore a strong prediction; if it fails, the failure mechanism would itself be a finding worth investigating.

The architectural claim being made — and the claim Category F bounds — is that the architecture supports late-appearing target location through its existing locating machinery. v0.13 does not introduce new locating capability. v0.13 demonstrates that the inherited capability handles a target whose location is unknown at design time and whose appearance is timed to all-attractors-mastered. Whether this constitutes a substantive architectural contribution depends on the framing of the question. As a stand-alone iteration, it is a bounded contribution: the existing machinery does what one would have predicted it would do. As a foundation for v0.14 / v0.15 work — where competency-gated content cells would also need to be located by an agent that does not know their coordinates in advance — it is a methodologically necessary precursor: v0.13 establishes that the locating mechanism works on the simpler case before v0.14 / v0.15 stresses it on the harder case.

## 9. Methodological note

This is a pre-registration for a new iteration, following the v0.12 pattern of design-conversation-before-pre-registration drafting. The four open considerations from the v0.13 design conversation (record of 25 April 2026, this session) were settled before this pre-registration was written:

- **The activation signal is all-attractors-mastered**, not mastery-rate-decline-window or any combination. The choice was made on the grounds that all-attractors-mastered is a structural fact tied to v0.11.2's convergence finding and does not introduce a tuning-threshold parameter that would compromise the architectural claim.
- **The behavioural response is end-state-cell-appearance**, not phase transition or run termination or drive-composition shift. The choice was made on the grounds that establishing the locating mechanism in isolation, with the cell's later behavioural role reserved for v0.14 / v0.15, preserves single-variable-change discipline and provides a foundation for the competency-gated content design discussed in the v0.12 design conversation.
- **The cell location is random-at-run-start**, not fixed. The choice was made on the grounds that a fixed location does not test whether the locating mechanism generalises across coordinates; random sampling at run start ensures the architectural claim is about locating capability rather than incidental coverage of a specific coordinate.
- **The Category F honesty constraint is named explicitly** to handle the inherited-dynamics-versus-v0.13-specific-capability question in advance, rather than retrofitting the architectural claim at interpretation time.

The decomposition of the design conversation into pre-registered choices is the methodological response to the v0.11 lesson and follows v0.12's practice. The implementation surface is larger than v0.12's was; whether the implementation completes in a single pass without amendment is the empirical question this pre-registration's commitments are designed to test.

The 20,000-step run length is included in the matrix despite the post-activation window distribution including some runs with insufficient remaining steps for the locating mechanism to succeed reliably. The reasoning is named in Section 1 of this pre-registration: time is essential to discovery and competency growth in this architecture, undefined-by-design rather than incidentally bounded. The 20,000-step Category A threshold is calibrated lower than 80,000 and 160,000 thresholds to acknowledge this; the run length is retained to make the run-length-dependence finding empirically testable rather than excluded from the matrix.

## 10. Stopping rule

If the v0.13 implementation produces an implementation-time discovery requiring further architectural change, a v0.13.1 amendment may be issued, with all v0.11.2 and v0.12 amendment-discipline conditions applying: amendment committed before amended code is written, single architectural change per amendment, full retention of original pre-registration in the public repository.

The amendment budget for v0.13 is fresh: three amendments within the iteration is the limit, beyond which the iteration's design itself is what needs revision. The v0.11 series exhausted its amendment budget at v0.11.2; the v0.12 iteration completed in a single pass without amendment. The v0.13 implementation surface is larger than v0.12's was, increasing the probability that implementation will surface something the design conversation has not anticipated. The fresh amendment budget is reasonable rather than an erosion of discipline because the design conversation has been thorough and the four open considerations have been settled before pre-registration.

If a v0.13.1 amendment is required, the question becomes whether the v0.13 architectural design is the right design for end-state target activation, not whether incremental amendments can resolve specific implementation issues. The stopping rule is named here to preserve this discipline against the temptation to treat amendments as a permitted means of saving the iteration's design.

## 11. Commitments

Three methodological commitments accompany this pre-registration:

First, no v0.13 code will be written before this pre-registration document is committed to the public repository at github.com/RancidShack/developmental-agent.

Second, the pre-registration specifies a single architectural goal — the addition of end-state target activation through random-location cell appearance on the all-attractors-mastered signal — implemented across the surface specified in Section 2. Any implementation-time discovery requiring further architectural change will trigger the amendment procedure with the conditions and stopping rule named in Section 10.

Third, the v0.13 batch results will be reported alongside v0.12 (for behavioural-preservation audit), v0.11.2 (for mastery-side baseline), and v0.10 (for threat-side baseline). The full inheritance arc from v0.10 → v0.11.2 → v0.12 → v0.13 is part of the research contribution: persistent threat representation (v0.10), bounded mastery (v0.11.2), category-level threat-side generalisation (v0.12), and end-state target activation (v0.13) as a programmatic answer to the unbounded-accumulation problem v0.9 identified, now extended to the question of what happens after the architecture has converged.
