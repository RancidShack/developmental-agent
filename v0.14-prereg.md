**Author:** Nicholas P M Baker, Synapstak Ltd
**Date:** 26 April 2026
**Status:** Pre-registration document, drafted before any v0.14 code is written

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 The arc the programme has now traced

A small tabular developmental agent occupying a 20×20 structured environment has, across five preceding iterations, acquired the architectural machinery a developmental learner needs to handle persistent categorical knowledge across two distinct cell types and to engage with new feature-reward sources that appear at random locations after the existing environment has been exhausted. v0.9 identified that the v0.8 architecture lacked persistent storage for any learned categorical knowledge (Baker, 2026c). v0.10 supplied it for hazards through experience-driven persistent threat representation (Baker, 2026d). v0.11.2 supplied it for mastery through the four mastery interventions (Baker, 2026g). v0.12 supplied category-level threat-side generalisation through cell-type signature-matching (Baker, 2026i, in preparation). v0.13 supplied late-target activation, demonstrating that the architecture's existing locating machinery handles new feature-reward sources at randomly-sampled locations through inherited Phase 3 dynamics (Baker, 2026l, in preparation).

By the close of v0.13 the architecture has, in a specific sense, completed the threat-and-mastery programme as originally scoped. The threat layer stores categorical hazard knowledge that generalises across signature-matched cells. The mastery layer stores categorical attractor knowledge bounded by the four mastery interventions. The agent reliably converges toward exhausting the available attractor set within the longest run-length tested, and when given a new target appearing at a random location at the moment of exhaustion, locates it at 93.9 per cent conditional rate at 160,000 steps. The architectural arc the programme has been tracing — from unbounded accumulation through bounded categorical representation to late-target activation — has reached its natural close on the threat-and-mastery side.

What the architecture has not yet done is engage with cell-type transformation tied to the agent's own developmental state. v0.13's end-state cell transitions from NEUTRAL to END_STATE on a structural fact about the architecture (`sum(mastery_flag.values()) == len(attractor_cells)`), and the cell's perceptual signature post-transition is attractor-equivalent. The transition is environment-side rather than experience-side: the same trigger fires identically across runs and the cell's properties post-transition are identical across runs. v0.13 establishes that the inherited locating machinery handles such transitions; it does not test whether the architecture handles transitions whose trigger depends on the agent's accumulated competency rather than on a structural milestone, or whose post-transition behaviour requires the existing threat layer's hard-gate machinery to acquire a competency-bounded exception.

### 1.2 What v0.14 is for

v0.14 is the iteration that closes the gap between the existing architecture and the competency-gated content reframing settled in the v0.12 design conversation (Baker, internal record, 24 April 2026). The reframing's central architectural commitment: hazard cells are not permanent obstacles but content gated behind competency thresholds, and the transformation from hazard to knowledge is the architecture's representation of the developmental shift from "I cannot enter this cell" to "I can now learn from this cell." The reframing requires three architectural elements the existing iterations do not provide and one that v0.13 has just established:

- **Competency thresholds attached to specific hazard cells.** The architecture must hold a per-cell threshold value and check the agent's accumulated competency against that threshold at every step. This is the v0.14-specific contribution at the architectural level.
- **A cell-type transition triggered by the agent's competency reaching the cell's threshold.** Unlike v0.13's all-attractors-mastered trigger (a structural milestone), v0.14's trigger is experience-dependent: the same seed produces different transition timings depending on the agent's accumulated learning trajectory. This is the v0.14-specific contribution at the developmental level.
- **A competency-override exception in the threat layer's hard-gate action selection.** v0.10's hard-gate excludes any action targeting a flagged cell from the action-selection pipeline. When a hazard cell transitions to a knowledge cell, the action-selection machinery must permit entry to the cell that was previously hard-gated, while the threat flag itself persists across the transition until the cell is banked as knowledge.
- **Locating machinery for the post-transition knowledge cell.** v0.13's iteration established that the inherited Phase 3 dynamics handle late-appearing attractor-equivalent cells at random locations. v0.14 inherits this capability: the post-transition knowledge cell carries `FEATURE_DRIVE_WEIGHT = 0.15` and `ATTRACTION_BONUS = 0.3`, parallel to attractor and end-state cells, and the existing locating machinery engages with it without v0.14-specific extension.

The fourth element is inherited and bounded by Category F (Section 7). The first three are the v0.14-specific architectural contributions whose operational success the present pre-registration commits to test.

### 1.3 The end-state cell composition with v0.14

v0.13's end-state cell is preserved unchanged in v0.14, with one architectural amendment to the activation signal. The original v0.13 trigger — `sum(mastery_flag.values()) == len(attractor_cells)` — fires when all six attractors are banked. Under v0.14, the architecture additionally requires that all five hazard cells with assigned competency thresholds have been banked as knowledge before the end-state cell activates. The amended trigger is `all_attractors_mastered AND all_hazards_banked_as_knowledge`. The architectural reasoning: the end-state cell is the architecture's representation of the developmental milestone "everything in this environment has been engaged with," and v0.14 introduces a new class of environmental content (knowledge cells) whose engagement should be required before the milestone fires. The amendment preserves v0.13's locating-mechanism finding while extending the milestone definition to include the new content class.

### 1.4 What this pre-registration commits to

The pre-registration commits, before any v0.14 code is written, to the following:

- The architectural specification for the v0.14 mechanism (Section 2), including the competency-threshold assignment to hazard cells, the competency calculation, the cell-type transition rule, the action-selection exception, the post-transition perceptual properties, the banking rule, and the amended end-state activation trigger.
- The experimental matrix and matched-seed comparison apparatus (Section 3), inheriting v0.13's batch design with seeds chained through v0.10 / v0.11.2 / v0.12 / v0.13.
- The metric set (Section 4), introducing per-cell competency-threshold tracking, transition timing, post-transition entry counts, and three pre-registered cross-layer-effect metrics.
- The pre-registered architectural predictions (Section 5), including the operational predictions for transition timing distributions, post-transition discovery rates, and the three cross-layer effects pre-registered as hypotheses.
- The interpretation categories (Section 6), with Category A discovery-rate thresholds calibrated against the v0.13 inheritance distribution rather than against absolute counts (per the v0.13.1 lesson, Section 5.4 of Baker, 2026l).
- The Category F honesty constraint (Section 7) bounded explicitly to inherited locating (v0.13) and inherited state-checking (the mastery flag dict from v0.11.2). v0.14's architectural contribution is limited to the competency-threshold mechanism and the cell-type transition; the locating and state-checking that operate on the transition are inherited.
- The amendment budget and stopping rule (Section 8).

The pre-registration document will be committed to the public repository at github.com/RancidShack/developmental-agent before any v0.14 code is written, in accordance with the methodological commitments named across the preceding iterations.

### 1.5 What this iteration does not address

Three architectural questions are explicitly out of scope for v0.14 and are reserved for subsequent iterations.

The richer-locating-mechanism question Section 5.2 of the v0.13 paper named — distinguishing the substantive reading (inherited machinery is general) from the deflationary reading (cell-type-matching artefact) — remains reserved for v0.15 or later. v0.14's post-transition knowledge cell carries attractor-equivalent perceptual properties on the same single-variable-simplification reasoning v0.13 applied: the simplest possible test of the new mechanism is to use perceptual properties identical to existing cells. v0.14 does not address whether the inherited locating handles perceptually distinct knowledge cells.

The mastery-side equivalent of v0.12's category-level generalisation — whether mastery on one attractor accelerates mastery of others — also remains reserved. v0.14's competency-threshold mechanism could in principle be extended into a signature-matching variant where competency on one knowledge cell propagates to others of the same category, but the present iteration tests the bare competency-gating mechanism on five cells with distinct thresholds rather than the propagation variant.

The v1.0 integration paper auditing the cumulative inheritance chain across v0.10 through v0.14 is reserved for subsequent work after the v0.14 batch and findings paper are complete.

## 2. Architectural Specification

### 2.1 Inherited architecture

v0.14 inherits the full v0.13 architecture unchanged except for the architectural extensions specified in Sections 2.2 through 2.6 below and the amended end-state activation trigger specified in Section 2.7. The threat layer operates as v0.10 / v0.12 specify: the v0.10 three-entry conversion rule applies to the first hazard cell encountered, the v0.12 signature-matching rule converts every subsequent hazard on first entry, and the hard-gate action-selection rule excludes any action targeting a flagged cell from the action-selection pipeline before Q-value computation, primitive bias computation, or epsilon-greedy random selection — except where the competency-override exception specified in Section 2.4 applies. The mastery layer operates as v0.11.2 specifies: the four mastery interventions hold for attractor cells (zero feature reward post-mastery, cleared attraction bias for mastered cells, preference reset at banking, blocked preference accumulation post-banking). MASTERY_THRESHOLD = 3 and MASTERY_BONUS = 1.0 are inherited unchanged. The three-phase developmental schedule, drive composition at each phase, learning rate 0.1, discount factor 0.9, and epsilon 0.1 are inherited unchanged. The pre-wired aversion bias of −5.0 for frame-adjacent actions is retained. The v0.13 end-state cell mechanism is inherited with the amended activation trigger specified in Section 2.7.

The single-variable-change discipline holds at the research-question level: v0.14 introduces competency-gated content transformation as the singular architectural extension. All prior architectural elements are preserved, modified only where the transformation mechanism requires the threat layer to acquire a competency-bounded exception (Section 2.4) or where the end-state activation trigger requires extension to include the new content class (Section 2.7).

### 2.2 Competency-threshold assignment

The five hazard cells in the environment — coordinates (5, 8), (5, 9), (6, 8), (14, 13), (14, 14) — are each assigned one competency threshold from the set {1, 2, 3, 4, 5}. The thresholds are integer values that the agent's accumulated competency must reach before the corresponding cell transitions from HAZARD to KNOWLEDGE.

The assignment is randomised per run. At run start, the architecture samples a permutation of {1, 2, 3, 4, 5} from the agent's NumPy random state and assigns the permutation's elements to the five hazard cells in the cell list's existing order. The sampling uses global RNG state save-and-restore parallel to v0.13's `_sample_end_state_cell` mechanism: the global RNG state is saved before the permutation draw, the draw is performed, and the state is restored. The architectural reasoning is methodological. v0.13 makes no `np.random` calls during world or agent construction beyond the end-state cell sampling, and v0.14's pre-activation behaviour audit (Section 6, Category A, Check 4) requires byte-identical pre-transition behaviour against v0.13 at matched seeds. A naive implementation of competency-threshold assignment without state-save-and-restore would consume RNG draws that v0.13 does not, shifting the agent's RNG state at first action selection and breaking the matched-seed guarantee. The implementation saves the state, performs the permutation draw, and restores the state. The threshold assignment is deterministic given the run's seed but does not advance the global stream.

The assignment is recorded per run in the metric set as `hazard_competency_thresholds`, a dictionary mapping cell coordinate to integer threshold value. The recording supports per-run analysis of transition timing and post-transition discovery as functions of which cell received which threshold.

### 2.3 Competency calculation

The agent's competency is a scalar value computed as `sum(self.mastery_flag.values())`, the count of attractor cells banked at the current step. The choice is deliberately the existing mastery dict — the same data structure v0.13's activation signal reads — rather than a new state representation. The architectural reasoning: introducing a new competency variable would create a v0.14-specific state element whose initialisation, update, and reset semantics would need separate specification and audit. Reading the existing mastery dict means competency is exactly the count of attractor cells the agent has banked, and the cell-type transition trigger reduces to a comparison between two integers (current mastery count and the cell's threshold) that the architecture is already capable of performing.

The Category F honesty constraint applies directly here. v0.14 contributes the cell-type transition rule and the action-selection exception, but the competency variable itself is the inherited mastery flag dict. The state-checking machinery — reading `sum(self.mastery_flag.values())` — is inherited from v0.13's activation check. v0.14 adds nothing to the state representation; what v0.14 adds is the use of that state to trigger cell-type transitions on a per-cell basis with cell-specific thresholds.

After every step, after `update_model` and `update_values` have completed for the current step and after v0.13's all-attractors-mastered check, the agent computes `current_competency = sum(self.mastery_flag.values())` and compares it against each hazard cell's threshold. Cells whose threshold has been reached but whose transition has not yet fired trigger transitions in the order the cell list iterates (which is fixed across runs given a fixed cell list); subsequent same-step transitions follow without intervening agent action.

### 2.4 Cell-type transition rule

When the agent's competency first reaches a hazard cell's assigned threshold, the cell transitions from HAZARD to KNOWLEDGE. The transition is performed by `world.transition_hazard_to_knowledge(cell)`, called by the agent's main loop at the step where the threshold is first reached. The method updates `self.cell_type[cell]` from HAZARD to a new constant `KNOWLEDGE = 5` and sets a flag `self.knowledge_unlocked[cell] = True`. The transition is recorded in the metric set: `competency_unlock_step[cell]` records the step at which the transition fires.

Three properties of the transition are architecturally consequential and committed here.

**Threat flag persistence.** The agent's threat flag for the cell, set during the v0.10 / v0.12 conversion process at first or subsequent hazard entry, persists across the cell-type transition. The flag is cleared only when the cell is banked as knowledge (Section 2.6). The architectural reasoning: the threat-layer state is experience-driven and represents the agent's accumulated knowledge that the cell carries hazard cost. Clearing the flag at the cell-type transition would erase this accumulated representation; the flag instead persists, and the action-selection exception (below) provides the competency-bounded permission for the agent to enter the previously-flagged cell.

**Competency-override exception in action selection.** v0.10's hard-gate rule excludes any action targeting a flagged cell from the action-selection pipeline. v0.14 introduces an exception: the hard-gate continues to exclude actions targeting cells where `threat_flag[cell]` is true AND `cell_type[cell] == HAZARD`, but does not exclude actions targeting cells where `threat_flag[cell]` is true AND `cell_type[cell] == KNOWLEDGE`. The exception is the competency-bounded permission described above; the agent retains the threat representation but is no longer prevented from acting on the cell. The implementation modifies `_is_action_excluded` (or its equivalent) to add the cell-type check.

**Pre-banking perceptual properties.** Between the cell-type transition and the cell being banked as knowledge, the cell carries `FEATURE_DRIVE_WEIGHT = 0.15` per visit and `ATTRACTION_BONUS = 0.3` for adjacent cells, parallel to attractor and end-state cells in their unbanked state. The values are identical to v0.13's end-state cell on the same single-variable-simplification reasoning (Section 1.5).

### 2.5 Locating machinery (inherited from v0.13)

The post-transition knowledge cell is a feature-rewarding cell whose location was known to the agent perceptually during the pre-transition period (the agent had encountered the cell as a hazard and acquired a threat flag for it). The locating problem is therefore weaker than v0.13's: the agent does not need to find a cell whose existence was previously invisible, only to engage with a cell whose hard-gate exclusion has lifted. The inherited Phase 3 dynamics — the novelty drive, learning-progress drive, primitive attraction bias, and preference accumulation — engage with the knowledge cell as they engage with any other feature-rewarding cell, without v0.14-specific extension.

The Category F honesty constraint applies to the locating capability v0.14 demonstrates. v0.13 established that the inherited machinery handles late-appearing attractor-equivalent cells at random locations; v0.14 inherits this capability at strictly weaker problem difficulty (the cell's location is already known). v0.14 contributes nothing to the locating mechanism. The post-transition discovery rate the v0.14 batch will report is therefore expected to approach or exceed v0.13's conditional discovery rates, bounded above by the same Phase 3 dynamics that bound v0.13. Any below-v0.13 conditional discovery rate would itself be a finding requiring explanation; any at-or-above rate is consistent with the inherited-machinery reading.

### 2.6 Banking rule

The knowledge cell banks after three post-unlock entries, parallel to attractor cells' MASTERY_THRESHOLD = 3. At banking, the four mastery interventions apply: feature reward depletes to zero, primitive attraction bias clears, cell preference resets to zero, and ongoing preference accumulation on the cell is blocked. Additionally, the cell's threat flag is cleared at banking, on the architectural reasoning that the developmental cycle from hazard through knowledge to banked-knowledge represents complete engagement with the cell, and the threat representation is no longer the operative state for the architecture's subsequent behaviour. The cell's `knowledge_banked[cell]` flag is set true, and the cell is added to the count consulted by the amended end-state activation trigger (Section 2.7).

The choice of three-entry banking rather than v0.13's one-entry banking is the natural symmetry with attractor cells. v0.13's one-entry banking was justified on the architectural reasoning that the cell is found, not mastered (Baker, 2026j, Section 6); v0.14's three-entry banking is justified on the converse reasoning that the knowledge cell's engagement is the architecture's representation of learning from the cell, which is the mastery-equivalent operation rather than the location-finding operation.

The pre-registration commits to three-entry banking explicitly here. Should the v0.14 batch produce evidence that the three-entry banking is too high a bar for reliable mastery within the post-unlock window — that is, should the conditional knowledge-banking rate fall below the post-unlock-entry rate by a substantial margin — the finding will be recorded honestly in the v0.14 paper as a result about the architecture's post-unlock engagement dynamics, not as grounds for post-hoc threshold revision.

### 2.7 Amended end-state activation trigger

v0.13's end-state cell mechanism is preserved unchanged with one amendment to the activation trigger. The original trigger was `sum(self.mastery_flag.values()) == len(self.world.attractor_cells)`. The amended trigger is `sum(self.mastery_flag.values()) == len(self.world.attractor_cells) AND sum(self.knowledge_banked.values()) == len(self.world.hazard_cells)`. The amended trigger fires when both all six attractors are banked and all five hazard cells have been banked as knowledge.

The architectural reasoning: the end-state cell is the architecture's representation of "everything in this environment has been engaged with." v0.14 introduces a new class of environmental content (knowledge cells), and the milestone is extended to include their banking. The end-state cell's perceptual properties post-activation, banking rule, and post-banking depleted state are preserved unchanged from v0.13.

The implication for the v0.14 batch: end-state activation in v0.14 is bounded above by the joint distribution of `all_attractors_mastered` and `all_hazards_banked_as_knowledge` within the run length. The activation rate will be lower than v0.13's at every run length, because v0.14's milestone is a strict extension of v0.13's. Section 6 calibrates the Category A threshold for v0.14 against this bounded inheritance distribution rather than against absolute counts (Section 5.4 of Baker, 2026l, methodological lesson).

## 3. Experimental Matrix and Matched-Seed Comparison

The experimental matrix is one architecture (v0.14) crossed with six cost levels (0.1, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 5.0, 10.0) crossed with three run lengths (20,000, 80,000, 160,000 steps) crossed with ten runs per cell, totalling 180 runs. The matrix matches v0.10, v0.11.2, v0.12, and v0.13 exactly.

For each (cost, run length, run index) triple, the random seed is matched to the v0.13 batch (Baker, 2026l, in preparation) at the same condition. Seeds are loaded from `run_data_v0_13.csv` rather than regenerated, on the technical reasoning named across the preceding iterations: Python's hash randomisation per session prevents deterministic seed regeneration, and the recorded seeds in the CSV are the data of record. The v0.13 batch's seeds were themselves loaded from the v0.12 CSV, which loaded from the v0.11.2 CSV, which contains both v0.10 and v0.11.2 architectures at matched seeds. The seed chain therefore links v0.10, v0.11.2, v0.12, v0.13, and v0.14 at every (cost, run length, run index) cell: same seed, same agent RNG state at construction, comparable trajectories up to the divergence each architecture introduces.

The matched-seed comparison produces a within-seed comparison up to the point at which the first competency threshold is reached. v0.14's behaviour in the period before the first cell-type transition fires — including all attractor mastery dynamics up to the first attractor banked, and any pre-first-transition threat-layer behaviour — is byte-identical to v0.13 at the same seed by construction. Divergence begins at the first transition step, at which point the previously-hard-gated cell becomes accessible and the agent's subsequent action selection differs from v0.13's. This temporal-scope operationalisation follows the v0.13.1 lesson directly (Baker, 2026k): byte-identical preservation audits must be restricted to the population and period where pre-change behaviour equals comparison-baseline behaviour.

The pre-registered scope of the byte-identical preservation audit (Check 4a in Section 6) is therefore "behaviour up to first attractor banking matches v0.13 at matched seeds." Beyond first attractor banking — at which point the lowest-threshold knowledge cell can transition — the comparison is no longer byte-identical by design, and divergence is characterised through the cross-layer effect audits (Section 5.3 and Check 4b) rather than tested for byte-identity.

## 4. Metrics

All v0.10, v0.11.2, v0.12, and v0.13 metrics are retained.

Seven metrics are introduced for v0.14, four primary and three derived for cross-layer effect characterisation.

**Hazard competency thresholds.** A dictionary mapping each hazard cell coordinate to its assigned threshold value from {1, 2, 3, 4, 5}, recorded at run start. Supports per-run analysis of transition timing as a function of which cell received which threshold.

**Competency unlock step.** A dictionary mapping each hazard cell coordinate to the step at which the cell's HAZARD-to-KNOWLEDGE transition fired, or null if the transition did not fire within the run length. The step value equals the step at which the agent's competency first reached the cell's threshold.

**Knowledge cell entries.** A dictionary mapping each hazard cell coordinate to the count of post-transition entries to the cell. Supports per-cell analysis of post-unlock engagement.

**Knowledge banked step.** A dictionary mapping each hazard cell coordinate to the step at which the cell was banked as knowledge (third post-transition entry), or null if the cell was not banked within the run length. The cell's threat flag is cleared at this step.

**Pre-transition hazard entries.** A dictionary mapping each hazard cell coordinate to the count of entries to the cell during the pre-transition period (when the cell was still HAZARD). This metric inherits from v0.10 / v0.12 hazard entry tracking but is split per-cell rather than aggregated across the hazard cluster, supporting per-cell analysis of how pre-transition exposure relates to post-transition engagement.

**Time to first transition.** Step at which the first knowledge cell transitions, equal to `min(competency_unlock_step.values())` over non-null values. This is the threshold-1 transition step in expectation, since the cell with assigned threshold 1 transitions at the step the first attractor is banked. The metric is the operational analogue of `time_to_first_mastery` in the mastery layer.

**Time to all transitions.** Step at which the last knowledge cell transitions, equal to `max(competency_unlock_step.values())` over non-null values. This is the threshold-5 transition step in expectation, fired at the step the fifth attractor is banked. The metric is the operational analogue of `time_to_final_mastery`.

The headline operational metric for v0.14 is the **knowledge banking rate**: the fraction of hazard cells across the batch that complete the full HAZARD → KNOWLEDGE → BANKED cycle within the run length. The diagnostic metric is the conditional banking rate: knowledge banking rate among transitioned cells only, conditioning on the precondition that the cell-type transition has fired.

The pre-registered cross-layer effect metrics for the audit specified in Section 5.3 are calculated on existing fields rather than introducing new ones: the audit compares per-run divergence on `hazards_flagged`, `time_to_first_flag`, `attractor_mastery_sequence`, `time_to_first_mastery`, `time_to_final_mastery`, `time_to_end_state_activation`, and `end_state_found_step` between v0.14 and v0.13 at matched seeds.

Per-run numerical data is preserved in CSV format; aggregate findings will be reported per cost level and per run length in the v0.14 findings paper.

## 5. Architectural Predictions

This section commits, before any v0.14 code is written, to the architectural predictions that the v0.14 batch is designed to test. The predictions are organised in three tiers: operational predictions about the v0.14 mechanism's direct behaviour (Section 5.1), inheritance predictions about how the v0.14 mechanism interacts with inherited dynamics (Section 5.2), and cross-layer effect hypotheses pre-registered as Category A audit checks (Section 5.3).

### 5.1 Operational predictions

**Transition timing distribution.** The five competency-threshold transitions fire at the steps `time_to_first_mastery` through `time_to_fifth_mastery` in each run, regardless of which cell received which threshold. The pre-registered prediction: the transition steps for thresholds 1 through 5 will match the agent's first-through-fifth attractor-mastery steps at matched seeds with v0.13, modulo any cross-layer effect on mastery timing (Section 5.3). The transitions are a deterministic readout of the agent's mastery trajectory; the architectural mechanism does not introduce new timing variation beyond the inherited mastery dynamics.

**Post-transition engagement.** Among transitioned knowledge cells, the architecture predicts post-transition engagement to occur at a high conditional rate. The reasoning: the post-transition cell carries attractor-equivalent perceptual properties (Section 2.4), the agent already has positional knowledge of the cell (it had encountered the cell as a hazard during the pre-transition period), and the inherited Phase 3 dynamics handle attractor-equivalent cells at high reliability when sufficient post-transition window remains. The pre-registered prediction: at 160,000 steps, the conditional first-entry-after-transition rate will exceed 90 per cent across transitioned cells, parallel to v0.13's 93.9 per cent conditional discovery rate at the same run length.

The prediction is bounded by an autonomy observation worth surfacing in advance. The cell-type transition makes the knowledge cell perceptually available and removes the hard-gate exclusion, but the post-transition feature reward and attraction bias values compete against — rather than override — the pull from not-yet-mastered attractors, the novelty gradient on cells the agent has not yet engaged with, learning-progress on in-flight trajectories, and accumulated preference for the path the agent was already on. A run in which the agent registers the transition but autonomously continues toward attractor 2 or 3 before engaging the newly-accessible knowledge cell is consistent with the architecture's drive composition operating as designed, and is the same feature-aligned preference formation signature the v0.8 paper named (Baker, 2026a). The post-transition delay distribution — the gap between `competency_unlock_step[cell]` and the cell's first post-transition entry — therefore reads two architectural properties depending on context: short delays in runs where competing attractors are exhausted are evidence of locating engagement, while longer delays in runs with active competing attractors are evidence of autonomous prioritisation rather than locating-mechanism weakness. The Section 4 metric set captures both, and the v0.14 findings paper will report the delay distribution split by competing-attractor presence rather than as a single conditional rate.

**Knowledge banking rate.** The three-entry banking threshold introduces a higher bar than v0.13's one-entry banking. The architecture predicts that the conditional banking rate will be lower than the conditional first-entry rate, with the gap reflecting cases where the agent enters the knowledge cell once or twice but does not complete three entries before run-length exhaustion. At 160,000 steps the conditional banking rate is predicted to exceed 75 per cent across transitioned cells; at 80,000 steps to exceed 60 per cent; at 20,000 steps to be substantially lower because the post-transition window for high-threshold cells is small or zero.

**End-state activation rate.** The amended trigger requires both `all_attractors_mastered` and `all_hazards_banked_as_knowledge`. The activation rate is bounded above by the joint distribution of these conditions within the run length. The architecture predicts: at 160,000 steps, end-state activation in 30 to 45 of 60 runs (lower than v0.13's 49 of 60, reflecting the additional condition); at 80,000 steps, 15 to 30 of 60 (lower than v0.13's 35 of 60); at 20,000 steps, 0 to 5 of 60 (substantially lower than v0.13's 13 of 60, reflecting that the threshold-5 transition cannot fire until the fifth attractor is mastered, leaving very little post-trigger window).

### 5.2 Inheritance predictions

**Mastery layer preservation.** Mastery dynamics are predicted to be preserved at full strength against v0.13 at matched seeds in runs where no cross-layer effect operates on mastery timing. The reasoning: v0.14 introduces no architectural change to the mastery layer, and competency-threshold transitions operate as readouts of the mastery dict rather than modifications to it. The Category A audit (Section 6, Check 4a) tests this prediction directly.

**Threat layer pre-transition preservation.** Threat-layer dynamics in the pre-transition period — defined as before the first competency-unlock step in each run — are predicted to be byte-identical to v0.13 at matched seeds. The reasoning: pre-first-transition behaviour involves no v0.14-specific code path, because the cell-type checks added in Section 2.4 reduce to v0.13 behaviour while all hazard cells remain HAZARD-typed. The Check 4a audit tests this scope-restricted preservation.

**End-state locating preservation.** Given activation, the end-state cell's locating performance is predicted to match v0.13's conditional discovery rates within run-length-comparable post-activation windows. The reasoning: v0.14 modifies the activation trigger but not the post-activation locating mechanism, and the inherited Phase 3 dynamics that handled v0.13's late-appearing target handle v0.14's identically. The prediction is bounded by Category F: the locating success transfers because the underlying mechanism is unchanged, and the v0.14 batch produces no new evidence about the locating mechanism's generality beyond what v0.13 established.

### 5.3 Cross-layer effect hypotheses (pre-registered as Category A audit checks)

The v0.13 paper documented two cross-layer behavioural effects across two iterations and named cross-layer effects as a phenomenon-class the programme can identify (Baker, 2026l, Section 4.3). The v0.14 iteration introduces three architectural extensions that plausibly produce cross-layer effects through the shared substrate of action selection. The pre-registration commits to three cross-layer effect hypotheses and pre-registers the matched-seed audit as a Category A check.

**Hypothesis 1: Competency-unlock affects threat-side encounter.** When a knowledge cell transitions, the previously-hard-gated cell becomes accessible to the agent's action selection, and the new feature-reward source pulls the agent's exploration trajectory toward it. The hypothesis predicts that the agent's subsequent encounter rate with other hazard cells (those still in HAZARD state) will diverge from v0.13 at matched seeds. The direction is not pre-registered — the effect could be bidirectional in the same way v0.13's end-state cell affected hazards-flagged in both directions across activated runs (Baker, 2026l, Table 2). The audit measures: per-run divergence on `hazards_flagged` and `time_to_subsequent_flag` between v0.14 and v0.13 at matched seeds, restricted to runs where at least one transition fires before the run-length boundary.

**Hypothesis 2: Knowledge-cell engagement affects subsequent attractor mastery.** The post-transition knowledge cells carry feature reward and attraction bias parallel to attractor cells. Multiple feature-rewarding cells active simultaneously (post-transition knowledge cells competing with not-yet-mastered attractors for the agent's attention) plausibly redistribute the agent's exploration in ways that affect when subsequent attractors are mastered. The hypothesis predicts that mastery timing fields beyond the first attractor will diverge from v0.13 at matched seeds in runs with multiple simultaneous feature-rewarding cells. The audit measures: per-run divergence on `time_to_second_mastery` through `time_to_final_mastery` between v0.14 and v0.13 at matched seeds, restricted to runs where the first transition fires before the second attractor is mastered.

**Hypothesis 3: End-state activation timing affects full-run cleanness.** The amended end-state activation trigger fires at a later step than v0.13's trigger in every activated run (because v0.14's trigger requires a strict superset of v0.13's conditions). The hypothesis predicts that full-run cleanness metrics — defined as the agent's Phase 3 trajectory and the resulting hazard encounter and end-state discovery rates — will diverge from v0.13 at matched seeds in runs that activate under both architectures. The audit measures: per-run divergence on `time_to_end_state_activation`, `end_state_found_step`, and Phase 3 hazard encounter counts between v0.14 and v0.13 at matched seeds, restricted to runs where end-state activation fires under both architectures.

The cross-layer effect audits are pre-registered as Category A checks. Their commitment is the matched-seed comparison apparatus and the per-run divergence count, not a pre-registered effect direction or magnitude. The v0.14 paper will report the audit results characteristically (counts and directional analysis) rather than testing them against pre-registered thresholds. The audit's purpose is to establish, in advance of the batch, that the v0.14 architecture's coupling to inherited dynamics will be characterised through matched-seed comparison rather than discovered post-hoc.

## 6. Interpretation Categories

Six interpretation categories are pre-registered. Categories A through E follow the v0.10 / v0.12 / v0.13 pattern with thresholds calibrated against the v0.13 inheritance distribution per the Section 5.4 lesson. Category F is the v0.14-specific honesty constraint.

### 6.1 Category A: successful competency-gated content transformation with full preservation

Category A is triggered when all five checks below are met.

**Check 1: Conditional first-entry-after-transition rate ≥ 85 per cent at 160,000 steps.** Among transitioned knowledge cells with at least 100 steps of post-transition window, the agent enters the cell at least once. The 85 per cent threshold is calibrated below v0.13's 93.9 per cent at 160,000 steps to reflect the modest expected reduction from competing feature-rewarding cells (Hypothesis 2). The threshold is calibrated against locating-mechanism engagement rather than against immediate engagement; per the autonomy observation in Section 5.1, runs in which the agent autonomously prioritises remaining attractors before engaging the transitioned cell are not Check 1 failures provided the cell is entered at any point within the post-transition window. A below-threshold result on Check 1 will be reported alongside the post-transition delay distribution split by competing-attractor presence, supporting interpretation of whether the shortfall reflects locating-mechanism weakness or autonomous prioritisation.

**Check 2: Conditional knowledge banking rate ≥ 75 per cent at 160,000 steps and ≥ 60 per cent at 80,000 steps.** Among transitioned knowledge cells, the agent completes three post-transition entries within the run length. Thresholds calibrated against the run-length-bounded post-transition window distribution.

**Check 3: End-state activation rate ≥ 30 of 60 at 160,000 steps.** End-state activation under the amended trigger fires in at least 30 runs at the longest run length. The threshold reflects the joint distribution prediction in Section 5.1; below this rate suggests the amended trigger is too restrictive for the post-activation window to remain operationally meaningful.

**Check 4a: Byte-identical preservation in the pre-first-transition period.** Behaviour up to first attractor banking matches v0.13 at matched seeds across all 17 audited fields, applied to the population of all 180 runs (because every run has a pre-first-transition period of non-zero length). The temporal-scope restriction follows the v0.13.1 lesson directly: byte-identical comparison is restricted to the period in which v0.14's architectural extension produces no behavioural effect.

**Check 4b: Cross-layer effect audits report characteristically.** The three cross-layer effect audits in Section 5.3 produce per-run divergence counts and directional analysis without exception. Check 4b does not test threshold conditions; it commits to the audit being performed and reported.

### 6.2 Category B: partial transformation

Category B is triggered when at least one Check 1, 2, or 3 threshold passes but not all three. Category B will be reported with explicit characterisation of which thresholds passed and which did not, supporting interpretation of which architectural elements (transition mechanism, banking mechanism, amended end-state trigger) succeeded operationally and which did not.

### 6.3 Category C: transformation mechanism failure

Category C is triggered when the conditional first-entry-after-transition rate at 160,000 steps falls below 50 per cent, or when the conditional knowledge banking rate at 160,000 steps falls below 30 per cent. Category C represents substantial below-threshold performance suggesting the transformation mechanism does not engage the inherited locating dynamics as predicted.

### 6.4 Category D: over-activation

Category D sub-conditions: any cell-type transition fires before the agent's competency reaches the cell's assigned threshold; any knowledge cell banks before three post-transition entries; the end-state cell activates before all hazard cells are banked as knowledge; the action-selection competency-override exception applies to a cell still in HAZARD state. Category D triggers if any sub-condition is observed in any run.

### 6.5 Category E: developmental degradation

Category E is triggered when Check 4a fails — when the byte-identical preservation audit, restricted to the pre-first-transition period, shows divergence from v0.13 at matched seeds. The temporal-scope restriction (per the v0.13.1 lesson) means Category E is triggered only by divergence the v0.14 architectural extension cannot have produced by design, which would indicate an implementation error rather than a behavioural property of the mechanism.

### 6.6 Category F: the honesty constraint

Category F is the v0.14-specific honesty constraint and applies whenever the operational result demonstrates competency-gated content transformation, regardless of whether Category A thresholds pass. The architectural claim made in any v0.14 paper is bounded by Category F as follows.

The v0.14 architectural contribution is the competency-threshold mechanism (Sections 2.2, 2.3) and the cell-type transition rule with action-selection exception (Sections 2.4, 2.6). What v0.14 does not contribute and inherits from preceding iterations: the locating machinery (inherited from v0.13's Phase 3 dynamics, themselves inherited from v0.11.2 / v0.12), the state-checking machinery (the mastery flag dict, inherited from v0.11.2), the threat representation (inherited from v0.10 / v0.12), the mastery banking machinery (inherited from v0.11.2's four interventions), and the end-state cell behaviour post-activation (inherited from v0.13).

If v0.14 succeeds at the operational level, the success is consistent with two architectural readings the v0.14 batch alone cannot distinguish. The substantive reading: the architecture supports content transformation tied to the agent's developmental state, and the inherited locating and state-checking machinery handle the new transformation mechanism without architectural modification. The deflationary reading: v0.14 added cells whose post-transition perceptual properties match attractor cells and whose transition trigger is a comparison between two integers the architecture already tracks, and the success is no more than a recombination of existing capabilities into a new behavioural surface. Both readings are consistent with the operational data the v0.14 batch will produce.

The v0.14 paper will commit to reporting the architectural claim within these bounds. The substantive reading's full support requires v0.15+ work testing competency-gated transformation with non-attractor-equivalent post-transition perceptual properties, or with state-checking that the architecture does not already perform. The present iteration tests the bare composition of inherited capabilities into the competency-gated content reframing; the architectural reading is bounded accordingly.

## 7. Methodology

### 7.1 Pre-registration discipline

This pre-registration document is committed to the public repository at github.com/RancidShack/developmental-agent before any v0.14 code is written. The repository commit log is the data of record for the pre-registration's timestamp; the document's content as committed is the version against which any subsequent amendment is measured.

The methodological commitment that has held across v0.10 through v0.13 holds here. The architectural specification, the experimental matrix, the metric set, the architectural predictions, and the interpretation categories are settled before implementation begins. Implementation may surface details that require operationalisation choices (the v0.13.1 amendment is the canonical example, and v0.14 explicitly inherits its temporal-scope-of-audit lesson in Section 3 and Check 4a). Operational amendments are permitted under the stopping rule below; architectural amendments are not.

### 7.2 Implementation sequence

The v0.14 implementation will proceed in a single pass without architectural amendment, following the v0.13 precedent. The implementation surface is comparable to v0.13's: a new cell type (KNOWLEDGE = 5), a new state representation (the per-cell threshold dict and the per-cell knowledge_banked dict), modifications to action selection (the competency-override exception in `_is_action_excluded`), modifications to perception and feature reward (the post-transition cells carry attractor-equivalent properties pre-banking), modifications to the agent's main loop (the per-step competency check and transition trigger), and the amended end-state activation trigger.

The sequence: the pre-registration is committed; `curiosity_agent_v0_14.py` is implemented in a single pass with comments referencing the relevant pre-registration sections at each architectural element; `curiosity_agent_v0_14_batch.py` runs the matrix specified in Section 3 with seeds loaded from `run_data_v0_13.csv`; the per-run CSV `run_data_v0_14.csv` and meta-report `meta_report_v0_14.txt` are generated; the audit logic implementing Check 4a (byte-identical, scoped to pre-first-transition period) and the three cross-layer effect audits (Check 4b) is applied to the resulting CSV; the v0.14 findings paper is drafted from the audit outputs.

### 7.3 Stopping rule and amendment budget

The amendment budget for v0.14 is three operational amendments. Architectural amendments are not permitted; if an architectural change becomes necessary during implementation, the iteration is halted and the architectural change is committed as v0.14.x with its own pre-registration document.

Operational amendments are amendments that clarify audit operationalisation, metric calculation, or implementation details that do not change the architectural specification. The v0.13.1 amendment is the canonical example: the audit operationalisation was clarified to restrict byte-identical comparison to non-activated runs without modifying the architecture. v0.14 inherits the v0.13.1 lesson directly in its temporal-scope restriction of Check 4a (Section 3, Section 6.1), so the lesson does not need to be re-learned.

The stopping rule: if more than three operational amendments are issued during the v0.14 iteration, the iteration is halted and the methodology surrounding pre-registration calibration is reviewed before any further iterations proceed.

### 7.4 Findings paper commitment

The v0.14 findings paper will be drafted from the v0.14 batch outputs and audit results. The paper will report the operational findings against Category A through E thresholds as pre-registered, the cross-layer effect audit results characteristically, the Category F honesty constraint applied to the operational claim, and the calibration discipline against v0.13 inheritance distributions named in Section 5.4 of Baker, 2026l.

The paper will follow the v0.13 paper's structural template: introduction with programme arc, methods with architectural specification and matched-seed comparison apparatus, results sections per pre-registered check, a dedicated section on cross-layer effects (parallel to Section 4 of the v0.13 paper) updating the phenomenon-class observation now across three iterations rather than two, and discussion sections covering the bounded architectural claim, the calibration discipline, the limits of the present work, the architectural questions the iteration opens, and the position of v0.14 in the broader programme arc.
