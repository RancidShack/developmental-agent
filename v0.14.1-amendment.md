# v0.14.1 Pre-Registration Amendment: Targeted Replication of Threshold-Assignment Effects

**Author:** Nicholas P M Baker, Synapstak Ltd
**Date:** 27 April 2026
**Amendment count:** 1 of 3 (per v0.14 pre-registration Section 7.3 stopping rule)
**Status:** Operational amendment, scoped to audit apparatus; no architectural change

---

## Scope and motivation

The v0.14 batch (run_data_v0_14.csv, completed 27 April 2026) produced the four findings characterised in the meta-report and in the working analysis log: Check 4a passes 180/180; the H2 cross-layer hypothesis on attractor mastery is overturned in direction (v0.14 masters more attractors than v0.13 in 49 runs vs 29 fewer); the end-state activation rate is higher in v0.14 than in v0.13 at every run length despite v0.14's amended trigger being a strict superset of v0.13's; the H1 hypothesis on threat-side encounter inverts twice and lands as a substantive cross-layer effect after correcting for the banking-clears-flag mechanism.

Per-cell, per-threshold analysis of the H2 inversion surfaced one signal that the batch alone cannot settle. The (cell=(5,9), threshold=1) condition produced a mean Δ(attractors_mastered) of +0.649 across 37 runs, with v0.14 mastering more attractors in 17 of 37 vs 4 in the other direction. Adjacent (cell=(5,9), threshold=2) and (cell=(5,9), threshold=3) conditions produced near-zero or negative deltas. The (cell=(14,14), threshold ≤ 3) conditions produced a clean obstacle-removal signature (mean +0.330 at low thresholds vs −0.015 at high thresholds), but the (5,9)+threshold-1 spike is sharper than the obstacle-removal mechanism alone predicts and may reflect a structural feature of the bottleneck cell + earliest-threshold combination.

n=37 at the (5,9)+threshold-1 condition is not large enough to settle whether the spike is structural or sampling noise from one particular permutation draw. A targeted replication with a different permutation seed addresses precisely this question.

## Operational specification

The replication is scoped narrowly:

1. **A second v0.14 batch is run with the same architecture and the same matched seeds, but with a different permutation draw for the hazard-competency-threshold assignment.** The agent seed loaded from `run_data_v0_13.csv` is unchanged. The mechanism for producing a different permutation while preserving the matched-seed comparison: an integer offset is added to the global RNG state immediately before the permutation draw in `_assign_hazard_competency_thresholds`, the permutation is sampled, and the saved RNG state (without offset) is restored. The post-restoration RNG state is exactly what the first batch produced, so the agent's first action-selection RNG state matches v0.13 to the bit.

2. **A separate output file is written: `run_data_v0_14_replication.csv`.** The first batch's CSV (`run_data_v0_14.csv`) is the pre-registered data of record and is not overwritten or modified.

3. **A standalone analysis utility is provided: `analyse_v0_14_replication.py`.** The utility loads both batches and produces a comparison table on the per-cell, per-threshold (Δ_attractors_mastered) metric, plus aggregate replication-vs-original figures on the four headline findings.

4. **The replication's results are reported in the v0.14 findings paper as a methodological note in the discussion section.** The replication is framed as targeted evidence on one specific per-cell pattern, not as a separate iteration. Findings hold or do not hold in the replication; either way the reporting is honest about what the targeted batch can and cannot settle.

## What this amendment does NOT do

The amendment does not modify the v0.14 architecture. The permutation-offset mechanism is implemented as a parameter on the world's `_assign_hazard_competency_thresholds` method (default offset of 0, behaviour identical to first-batch v0.14); the architecture is unchanged.

The amendment does not modify the v0.14 pre-registration's interpretation categories (A through F). The first batch's results stand against those categories as pre-registered.

The amendment does not modify the v0.14 batch's matched-seed comparison apparatus against v0.13. Check 4a passes in the first batch (180/180); the replication batch will pass identically by construction (the permutation-offset mechanism preserves the global RNG state at first action selection).

## What the amendment commits to honest reporting on

Three outcomes are possible in the replication, all of which are reported honestly:

- **Replication pattern matches the first batch.** The (5,9)+threshold-1 spike persists with a similar magnitude (mean Δ ≥ +0.4 across an equivalent n). The structural reading of obstacle-removal at the bottleneck cell + earliest threshold gains support.
- **Replication pattern differs from the first batch.** The (5,9)+threshold-1 spike weakens or disappears. The first-batch finding is treated as one sample from a noisier underlying distribution; the per-cell analysis is reported with the appropriate noise caveat.
- **Replication produces a new (cell, threshold) spike at a different combination.** The per-cell pattern is variable across permutation draws and the obstacle-removal reading at the per-cell level is not stable. The aggregate H2 inversion still holds, but the per-cell mechanism story is more complex than either reading currently offers.

Whichever outcome occurs, the v0.14 findings paper reports it transparently as a result of the targeted replication, not as evidence for or against the pre-registered Category A through F verdicts on the first batch.
