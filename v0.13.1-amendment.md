# v0.13.1 Pre-Registration Amendment: Audit Scope Clarification

**Author:** Nicholas P M Baker, Synapstak Ltd
**Date:** 25 April 2026
**Status:** Amendment to v0.13-preregistration.md, committed before any v0.13.1 code change is made
**Inheritance baseline:** v0.13 (Baker, 2026j)
**Preceding work:** v0.13 pre-registration (Baker, 2026j), v0.13 batch (Baker, internal record, 25 April 2026)

---

## 1. Reason for amendment

The v0.13 batch (180 runs, matched seeds against the v0.12 batch CSV) executed cleanly and produced the substantive findings the iteration was designed to test. Conditional discovery rate given activation reached 93.9% at 160,000 steps (46 of 49 activated runs successfully located the end-state cell), 82.9% at 80,000 steps, and 46.2% at 20,000 steps. The locating mechanism the iteration was designed to demonstrate operates as predicted when the activation precondition is met. The Category D over-activation checks both passed at 0/180 — activation never fires before all six attractors are mastered, the end-state cell is never found before activation. The v0.12 signature-matching invariant (first_entry == n_flagged - 1) holds in 100% of flagged runs across the batch, confirming threat-layer inheritance is preserved.

One pre-registered check did not pass as written: the byte-identical pre-activation audit (Section 7 Check 4 of the v0.13 pre-registration). 132 of 180 runs matched the v0.12 baseline byte-for-byte; 48 diverged. Investigation of the divergence pattern (audit script in lab record, 25 April 2026) revealed that the audit-as-implemented was confounding two distinct questions:

- **The original methodological intent of Check 4:** verify that v0.13's architectural extension does not contaminate pre-activation behaviour. The expectation: the agent's behaviour from step 0 up to the activation step (or full-run, if activation does not fire) is byte-identical to v0.12 at matched seeds.
- **What Check 4 actually measured:** end-of-run aggregate metrics, which include post-activation behaviour for activated runs.

Five facts characterise the divergence pattern and clarify the issue:

1. **All 48 divergent runs activated.** Among 97 activated runs across the batch, 48 (49.5%) diverged. Among 83 non-activated runs, 0 diverged.
2. **Divergence is exclusively threat-layer.** All 48 divergent runs show threat-only divergence; 0 mastery-only, 0 both-categories. Mastery dynamics are byte-identical between v0.13 and v0.12 at every matched seed.
3. **Activation can fire during Phase 2.** In activated runs at 20,000 steps, mean activation step is 9,550 and Phase 2 ends at step 12,000; in 80,000-step runs, mean activation 23,832 versus Phase 2 end at 48,000. Once activation fires, the end-state cell becomes feature-rewarding and the agent's trajectory diverges from v0.12 from that step onward — including any remaining Phase 2 steps.
4. **Threat-layer fields recorded as full-run aggregates capture post-activation entries.** `hazard_entries_p2`, `hazards_flagged`, `time_to_final_flag`, `first_entry_conversions` all include events that may have occurred after the activation step but before the end of Phase 2 or the end of the run.
5. **End-state cell location is not the divergence driver.** Divergent runs are *less* likely to have end-state cells adjacent to hazards (2.1% versus 6.8% in matching runs), ruling out a simple cell-position confound.

The architectural change v0.13 introduces did exactly what the design conversation specified: pre-activation, the end-state cell exists as a neutral cell with no architectural effect; post-activation, the cell becomes feature-rewarding and pulls the agent's attention. The audit failure does not reflect contamination of pre-activation behaviour. It reflects the audit's operationalisation as full-run aggregates rather than as pre-activation slices, and the architectural fact that activation in many runs occurs during Phase 2.

This amendment clarifies the audit scope so that the audit measures what Section 7 Check 4 was committing to, and surfaces the post-activation threat-layer divergence as a substantive empirical finding to be reported in the v0.13 paper rather than as a Category E (developmental degradation) signal.

## 2. The amendment

Section 7 Check 4 of the v0.13 pre-registration is amended as follows.

### 2.1 Original text (Section 7, Category A, Check 4)

> Behavioural preservation: pre-activation behaviour byte-identical to v0.12 at matched seeds across all 180 runs. Specifically: hazard entries by phase, hazards flagged, time to first flag, time to second flag, time to final flag, first-entry conversions, attractors mastered, time to first mastery, time to final mastery, mastery sequence, and first-banked attractor identity must all match v0.12's recorded values for the same (cost, run length, run index) triple, up to the activation step.

### 2.2 Amended text (Section 7, Category A, Check 4)

The amendment splits Check 4 into two sub-checks, the first of which the original text was operationalising correctly (full-run byte-identical for runs where pre-activation equals full-run) and the second of which the original text intended but did not operationalise (the post-activation cross-layer-effect characterisation).

**Check 4a — pre-activation byte-identical, restricted to non-activated runs.** For runs in which `activation_step is None` — runs in which the architecture did not reach all-attractors-mastered within the run length, and therefore the architectural extension v0.13 introduces had no behavioural effect — full-run metrics must be byte-identical to v0.12 at matched seeds. The metrics audited are those listed in the original Check 4 text: hazard entries by phase, hazards flagged, time to first flag, time to second flag, time to final flag, first-entry conversions, attractors mastered, time to first mastery, time to final mastery, mastery sequence, first-banked attractor identity.

The audit pass condition: 100% of non-activated runs match v0.12's recorded values across all listed metrics.

**Check 4b — post-activation cross-layer effect, characterised.** For runs in which activation fires (`activation_step is not None`), divergence from v0.12 in post-activation Phase 2 / Phase 3 dynamics is *expected* by the architecture as designed: the activated end-state cell is a new feature-reward source whose attentional pull legitimately alters the agent's subsequent trajectory. The audit operationalisation in this population is therefore not byte-identical comparison; it is characterisation of the cross-layer effect. The metrics characterised are: divergence rate from v0.12 baseline (count of activated runs where any audited field differs); direction of divergence (does v0.13 typically flag more hazards than v0.12 or fewer); field-level divergence pattern (which threat-layer metrics most often differ); and any structural correlates (does end-state cell location, activation step, or post-activation window predict divergence).

The reporting condition: the v0.13 paper reports the post-activation threat-layer divergence as a substantive cross-layer finding parallel to the v0.11.2-mastery-affects-threat-side observation surfaced in the v0.12 paper. Category E (developmental degradation) is not triggered by Check 4b alone; it would only be triggered if mastery-side metrics also diverged in the activated-runs population, which they do not in the v0.13 batch.

### 2.3 Implementation surface

The amendment is operational, not architectural. No code change to `curiosity_agent_v0_13.py` is required. The change is to the audit logic in `curiosity_agent_v0_13_batch.py`'s meta-report generation block, restricting the byte-identical comparison to non-activated runs and adding a separate characterisation block for activated runs.

The original audit code remains useful for diagnostic purposes — it identifies which runs diverge — but its result is not the basis for the Category A Check 4 verdict. The amended audit produces two separate verdicts: byte-identical pass/fail on non-activated runs, and a descriptive characterisation of the activated-runs population.

## 3. Predicted result of the amended audit

On the existing v0.13 batch CSV, the amended audit is predicted to produce:

- **Check 4a:** 83 of 83 non-activated runs match v0.12 byte-for-byte. Pass.
- **Check 4b:** 48 of 97 activated runs diverge from v0.12 on at least one threat-layer field. Direction analysis: v0.13 records higher hazards_flagged in 17 runs versus v0.12 higher in 11; higher first_entry_conversions in 17 runs versus 10. The pattern suggests the end-state cell's attentional pull modestly increases hazard encounter rate in activated runs. Mastery-side metrics do not diverge in any activated run.

These predictions are derived from analysis of the v0.13 batch CSV as committed (lab record, 25 April 2026); they are committed to in this amendment as the result of the amended audit on the existing data.

## 4. What the amendment does not change

The architectural specification of v0.13 in Section 2 of the original pre-registration is unchanged. The activation signal, the end-state cell type, the cell sampling logic, the activate_end_state method, the banking rule, the inheritance from v0.12 — all remain as specified in the original document. The implementation in `curiosity_agent_v0_13.py` is unchanged.

The Category A discovery-rate thresholds (30/60 at 20k, 50/60 at 80k, 55/60 at 160k) in Section 7 of the original pre-registration are unchanged by this amendment. Whether those thresholds are met by the v0.13 batch is a separate question, addressed in the v0.13 paper. The amendment does not relax any threshold.

The Category F honesty constraint in Section 7 of the original pre-registration is unchanged. The architectural claim remains bounded: v0.13's locating capability operates through inherited Phase 3 dynamics; the v0.13 contribution is the cell type, the activation signal, and the cell-type transition.

The Category D over-activation checks in Section 7 of the original pre-registration are unchanged. The v0.13 batch passes both Category D sub-conditions at 0/180.

## 5. Methodological note

This amendment surfaces a methodological lesson worth recording explicitly. The original Check 4 text named "pre-activation byte-identical" but the implementation operationalised this as "full-run aggregates byte-identical." The two are equivalent for non-activated runs but materially different for activated runs in which activation occurs before the end of Phase 2 or in which post-activation steps occur within the same run-length-aggregated metric.

The lesson, for future iterations: when committing to byte-identical preservation across an architectural change that introduces mid-run cell-type transitions or other state changes that affect ongoing trajectory, the audit must operationalise the temporal scope of the comparison precisely. Aggregate metrics that span the architectural-change moment cannot be used as byte-identical-preservation evidence; they must be split into pre-change and post-change components, or the comparison must be restricted to runs in which the architectural change does not fire.

This lesson generalises beyond v0.13. v0.14 / v0.15 work on competency-gated content (the design framework that emerged from the v0.12 design conversation, recorded in lab notes 25 April 2026) will involve similar mid-run cell-type transitions: hazard cells transforming to knowledge cells when competency thresholds are met. Any preservation audit for those iterations will need to handle the same temporal-scoping issue this amendment surfaces.

## 6. Stopping rule re-instatement

The original pre-registration's Section 10 stopping rule is re-instated. The amendment budget for v0.13 is now at one of three. Two further amendments remain available within the iteration; beyond three, the iteration's design is what needs revision rather than incremental amendment.

The current amendment qualifies as an architectural-discipline amendment (parallel to v0.11.1 and v0.11.2) only in the sense that it commits to a methodological clarification before the v0.13 paper is drafted. It does not modify the architecture. If subsequent amendments are required, they should be reserved for genuine architectural discoveries.

## 7. Commitments

Three methodological commitments accompany this amendment:

First, this amendment is committed to the public repository at github.com/RancidShack/developmental-agent before any v0.13 batch re-analysis or v0.13 paper drafting begins.

Second, the amended audit logic in `curiosity_agent_v0_13_batch.py` is committed alongside this amendment. The implementation is operational only; no architectural change is made.

Third, the v0.13 paper will report both Check 4a (pre-activation preservation, restricted to non-activated runs) and Check 4b (post-activation cross-layer effect, characterised) as separate findings. The audit-as-implemented misnaming is reported transparently as a methodological observation in the paper's discussion section.
